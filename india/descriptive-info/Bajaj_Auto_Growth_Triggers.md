## 1. COMPANY PROFILE

Bajaj Auto Limited is India's second-largest two-wheeler manufacturer and the world's largest motorcycle exporter. Products: Pulsar, Avenger, CT, Dominar, KTM/Triumph (premium), Chetak (EV scooter), RE/Maxima (3-wheelers, CNG+EV). Subsidiaries: BACL (NBFC), KTM AG (consolidated from November 2025). FY26 Standalone: Revenue INR 58,732 Cr (+17% YoY), EBITDA INR 12,019 Cr (+19%, 20.5% margin), PAT INR 9,825 Cr (+21% YoY). FY26 Consolidated: Revenue INR 62,905 Cr (+23% YoY), PAT INR 10,744 Cr (+47% YoY). Volumes: 51.17 lakh units — first time crossing 5 million mark.

## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: Exports — USD 2.2 Billion Revenue (Highest-Ever), 22.5 Lakh Units

FY26 export volumes: 22.5 lakh units (+21% YoY). Revenue: USD 2.2 billion — highest ever. Latin America: 3rd consecutive record. Africa: recovering. Asia: double-digit growth. CV exports: +49% YoY. Q4 FY26: Exports 6 lakh+ units for 2nd consecutive quarter (+25% YoY). Target: 2.2 lakh units/month.

**Evidence**
- Q4 FY26 call (Dinesh Thapar, CFO): "Exports BU crossed 6 lakh units for 2nd consecutive quarter, 25% YoY. Highest-ever quarterly export revenue."
- Press release: "Export volumes 22.5 lakh units (+21% YoY). Revenue USD 2.2 billion — highest ever."

---

### Driver 2: Electric Vehicles — EBITDA Neutral Achieved, 3W EV Leadership

EV portfolio: INR 8,000+ Cr revenue, >20% of domestic sales. Chetak: 1 lakh+ quarterly retail in Q4 FY26. Revenue INR 4,000+ Cr FY26. Chetak C25 launched (youth/last-mile). EV 3-wheelers: 49% volume growth FY26. Bajaj is #1 in electric 3-wheelers. E-scooter segment +40% Q4 FY26; +60%+ in April FY27.

**Evidence**
- Q4 FY26 call: "EV portfolio hit double-digit EBITDA margin for first time. Chetak reached EBITDA neutral as a portfolio."
- Q4 FY26 call: "E-scooter segment grew 40% in Q4 and 60%+ in April."

---

### Driver 3: Pulsar Sports Segment — 10 New Variants, Share Gains in All States

150cc+ fastest-growing segment in India. 10 new Pulsar variants/upgrades in Oct–Mar 2026; contributing 50% of Pulsar sales. N/NS Pulsar series: growing at 2x industry rate in Q4. Pulsar brand revenue: ~INR 17,000 Cr FY26 across domestic + export.

---

### Driver 4: KTM AG Acquisition — INR 5,000 Cr Revenue, Consolidated from November 2025

Bajaj acquired controlling stake in KTM AG (KTM, Husqvarna, GasGas brands) in November 2025. KTM/Triumph global revenue: ~INR 5,000 Cr (+40% YoY in FY26). 2.25 lakh unit annual volume. FY27: First full-year consolidation = meaningful inorganic revenue boost.

**Evidence**
- Economic Times: "KTM–Triumph global revenue ~INR 5,000 crore (+40% YoY). Portfolio expansion across KTM Duke/Adventure and Triumph modern classics."

---

### Driver 5: BACL (Bajaj Auto Credit) — PAT 12x to INR 665 Crore

FY26: Total income INR 3,000+ Cr. PAT INR 665 Cr (vs INR 58 Cr FY25 — 12x increase). Supports EV 3-wheeler sales via new business and refinancing models. 100+ city electric rickshaw presence.

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. Commodity inflation: 3.5–4% in Q4 FY26. 40% offset via pricing; rest absorbed. FY27 margin risk.
2. Middle East geopolitics: LPG supply constraints, freight costs +20–50% on some routes.
3. Nigeria export disruption: Key Africa market currency/regulatory risk.
4. BACL credit quality: NPA risk as 3-wheeler EV repayment history matures.
5. KTM turnaround uncertainty: "Well underway" but not at full performance.
6. EV competition: Ola Electric, TVS iQube competing on Chetak's turf.
