## 1. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: NGC (Next Generation Corvette) — INR 25,000–33,000 Crore Near-Term Order

**What is the driver?**
The NGC programme is GRSE's single most significant near-term catalyst. 8 ships total (~INR 40,000 crore). GRSE is L1 for 5 ships (~INR 25,000–33,000 crore). GSL gets 3 ships. CCS (Cabinet Committee on Security) approval is the final pending step.

**Mechanism**
- Current order book (December 31, 2025): INR 18,482 crore
- Post-NGC: Management expects order book ~INR 50,000 crore
- Revenue timing: NGC production revenue from Q4 FY28 (2-year design + engineering phase)
- First stage payment (advance): Expected in Q4 FY26
- Once execution begins: INR 25,000–33,000 crore over 8–10 years = INR 2,500–4,000 crore/year

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 analyst meet (CMD PR Hari, February 11, 2026): "By the end of this financial year, we should have the NGC contract. First stage payment expected during the current financial year. Order value is in the order of plus INR 30,000 crore."
- Stockaxis (April 22, 2026): "Contract negotiations completed; final CCS approval pending. Management expects order book to scale to ~INR 50,000 crore post NGC signing. Revenue from NGC from Q4 FY28 onwards."
- Defence.in (March 24, 2026): GRSE (5 ships, ~INR 25,000 crore) + GSL (3 ships). Physical construction to begin after CCS approval.

**Time Horizon:** Imminent (FY26/FY27 order) — Revenue from Q4 FY28.

### Driver 2: Current Order Book Execution — Record Revenue in Progress

**What is the driver?**
9M FY26 revenue INR 4,883 crore — already approaching full-year FY25 (INR 5,076 crore). Q3 FY26 is strongest-ever quarterly revenue. Execution is at highest pace in company history.

**Key Physical Milestones (9M FY26)**
- 5 major platforms delivered: 1x P-17A Frigate, 3x ASWSCs, 1x Survey Vessel (~1 ship every 2 months)
- 3 more major vessels targeted for delivery in Q4 FY26
- 10 active projects, 42 platforms

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 results (January 27, 2026): Revenue INR 1,896 crore (+49% YoY). EBITDA INR 234 crore (+59% YoY). PAT INR 171 crore (+74% YoY).
- 9M FY26: Revenue INR 4,883 crore (+42%). PAT INR 445 crore (+57%). EBITDA INR 643 crore (+53%).
- CMD (Q3 analyst meet): "Order book is below INR 20,000 crore for the first time — good news. Our execution rate has picked up. We are well on track for best-ever financial performance."

**Time Horizon:** Immediate (FY26 and FY27 transition).

---

### Driver 3: Gujarat Greenfield Shipyard — Capacity Expansion + Commercial Diversification

**What is the driver?**
GRSE planning new greenfield shipyard in Gujarat — addressing Kolkata yard capacity constraints and opening access to western coast demand (merchant ships, offshore vessels, commercial vessels).

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 analyst meet: "Expansion plans include new facilities in Gujarat for both defense and non-defense sectors."
- February 9, 2026: GRSE + HSL (Hindustan Shipyard) signed MoU for "large-scale nationally significant shipbuilding programme consortium."

**Time Horizon:** Long term (3–5 years) — Greenfield planning stage.

---

### Driver 4: Non-Defence Diversification — Portable Steel Bridges + Naval Weapon Systems

**What is the driver?**
GRSE diversified beyond warships into portable steel bridges (NHAI, military engineering, border connectivity) and naval weapon systems (gun mounts, torpedo launchers, stabilised platforms).

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 (CMD): GRSE active in "ship repairs, portable steel bridges, and naval weapon systems."
- These provide revenue continuity between major ship deliveries.

**Time Horizon:** Ongoing and growing — not a step-change but steady incremental revenue.

---

### Driver 5: Export Orders — Indian Ocean Region Naval Market

**What is the driver?**
GRSE has delivered ships to Mauritius and Sri Lanka. Discussions with IOR countries (Southeast Asia, Africa, Middle East) for patrol vessels, fast attack craft, logistics ships.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Export ships form part of INR 18,482 crore order book. Specific values not separately disclosed.
- Government-to-government defence deals increasingly facilitating export orders.

**Time Horizon:** Ongoing and building.

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. **Revenue gap FY27**: P-17A nearing completion; NGC production revenue only from Q4 FY28. Potential softer FY27.
2. **NGC signing delay**: CCS approval required — could slip if political/fiscal priorities intervene.
3. **Kolkata yard capacity**: 10 active projects, 42 platforms — very high utilisation; any resource crunch delays deliveries.
4. **EBITDA margin limitation**: 12–13% EBITDA margin is sector standard for shipbuilding; limited upside vs BEL/BDL/Data Patterns.
5. **Steel cost escalation**: Shipbuilding is 30–40% steel by cost. Price cycles can erode fixed-price contract margins.
