# Amagi Media Labs (AMAGI)

### Growth Triggers

**Cloud Modernization — Secular migration from hardware to SaaS.** Amagi's foundational trigger is a structural platform migration: only ~10% of global TV channels have moved to cloud infrastructure, leaving a **$16.9 billion TAM** still predominantly hardware-based. Every incremental broadcaster moving to the cloud is a customer acquisition opportunity for Amagi's glass-to-glass platform. The company already runs marquee live events — Super Bowl, Winter Olympics, Grammys — on its infrastructure, which serves as a powerful proof-of-quality signal for enterprise customers.[^15]

**Streaming Unification — Geographic expansion beyond the US.** Approximately **90% of revenue** comes from the US and Europe today, but management said in Q3 that they are seeing streaming adoption accelerating in **Latin America and Asia Pacific** — and India is explicitly described as the fastest-growing market. The company now serves 499 customers across 40+ countries and distributes content to 408+ platforms (up from 350+ at DRHP filing).[^16][^17][^15]

**Monetization — Ad impression volumes at inflection.** Ad impressions delivered grew **~60% YoY** to 13 billion in Q3 FY26, and content hours ingested on the platform grew **64% YoY to ~8 lakh hours**. Both are leading indicators that existing customers are deepening their platform usage, consistent with the NRR of **127%** reported at the H1 FY26 level.[^18][^15]

**AI as the next TAM expansion.** Management characterized AI as the **single biggest investment area** for FY27 onward. The opportunity is twofold: (1) productivity improvement for media companies (reducing human-intensive operations); and (2) AI-enabled agentic tools within the Amagi platform (already in design-partner/POC stage with multiple customers). Given that media is historically one of the most capital and labor-intensive industries, the AI productivity angle is a meaningful value proposition for upselling existing customers.[^15]

**40+ new customer logos in the last 12 months** were added, supporting future NRR-driven compounding.[^16][^15]

### Revenue and Margin Evolution

| Period | Revenue | YoY | Adj. EBITDA | Adj. EBITDA Margin | PAT |
|--------|---------|-----|------------|-------------------|-----|
| 9M FY25 | ₹851 cr | — | ₹10 cr | ~1% | Loss |
| Q3 FY26 | ₹404 cr | +22% | ₹58 cr | 14.3% | ₹31 cr[^16] |
| 9M FY26 | ₹1,109 cr | +30% | ₹116 cr | 10.5% | ₹37 cr[^16] |

The operating leverage story is the core margin trigger. Operating costs grew **14–17%** against revenue growth of **22–30%**. Two-thirds of Amagi's cost base is fixed or semi-fixed (employees + other operating); only cloud infrastructure costs (~communication) scale linearly with revenue. This structural cost architecture means every incremental rupee of revenue flows disproportionately to EBITDA.[^15]

**Management guidance on steady-state margins:** Q3 reported adj. EBITDA margin of 14.3% included seasonal advertising tailwinds. Management guided that a normalized Q3 steady-state margin is closer to **~10%**, and that long-term margin should approach ~25% (vertical SaaS benchmarks), with leverage coming from declining S&M and R&D as a percentage of revenue.[^15]

**Cash:** Amagi ended Q3 with ₹803 crore cash + investments, including IPO proceeds from the January 2026 listing (₹1,789 crore raise). All R&D and AI investment is expensed, not capitalized.[^16][^15]

**Note on Q3 accounting timing:** One top-5 customer's perpetual license revenue (per Ind AS channel go-live recognition) was all recognized in H1 FY26 versus a pro-rata spread in FY25. This creates a 3–4% tailwind in H1 and equivalent headwind in H2. Underlying Q3 growth, adjusted for this, would be **25–27%**.[^15]
