## 1. COMPANY PROFILE

Solar Industries India Limited is India's largest manufacturer of industrial explosives (bulk, cartridge, emulsion) and a rapidly growing defense ammunition manufacturer. Business: (1) Industrial explosives — mining, infrastructure, quarrying (India and 9 international manufacturing countries); (2) Defence — ammunition, rockets (Pinaka), artillery shells, torpedoes, hand grenades, aerial bombs, warheads. Revenue: INR 8,785 crore (9M FY26 extrapolated). Defence segment: 28% of revenue and accelerating rapidly. EBITDA margins: 28–29%.

## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: Pinaka Rocket System — INR 6,084 Crore Contract + INR 10,000 Crore CCS-Cleared Order

**What is the driver?**
Pinaka is India's indigenous multi-barrel rocket launch system (MBRL) — equivalent of BM-21 Grad/HIMARS. Solar Industries' subsidiary EEL is DRDO's designated production agency for Pinaka rockets. This is Solar's single largest ever contract.

**Mechanism**
- Contract 1 (February 2025): INR 6,084 crore from Ministry of Defence for Area Denial Munition (ADM) Type-1 and HEPF Mk-1 rockets. Execution: 8–15 years (86% in first 10 years).
- Contract 2 (January 2025, CCS-cleared): INR 10,000 crore Pinaka order cleared — Solar + MIL sharing (INR 5,700 crore HE ammunition + INR 4,300 crore ADM). Solar's share: ~INR 5,000–6,000 crore.
- Total Pinaka exposure: ~INR 11,000–12,000 crore over 8–10 years.
- Commercial dispatches: Q4 FY26 (guided).
- Revenue visibility: INR 1,100–1,500 crore/year for 8 years from Pinaka alone.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q2 FY26 (October 2025): "Defence segment revenue crossed INR 500 crore." Pinaka negotiations in final stages.
- Q3 FY26 (February 2026): "Defence revenue INR 702 crore (+72% YoY). Pinaka deliveries to begin Q4 FY26."
- Q3 FY26 call: "Management maintains INR 3,000 crore defence revenue guidance for FY26. Pinaka dispatches Q4."
- ICICI Direct (November 2025): Pinaka order in final commercial negotiation — expected in near term.

**Time Horizon:** Immediate (Q4 FY26 first dispatches) — major revenue ramp from FY27.

### Driver 2: 155mm Artillery Shells — Global Shortage + Nagpur Facility

**What is the driver?**
Global shortage of 155mm artillery shells (Ukraine war demand, NATO restocking) + India's own Army needs. Solar Industries building India's — and potentially world's — largest 155mm shell manufacturing facility in Nagpur.

**Mechanism**
- NATO/Ukraine demand: Post-Russia-Ukraine conflict, global inventory of 155mm shells severely depleted. US/Europe paying premium for any manufacturer with capacity.
- India Army requirement: India itself importing 155mm shells; Solar's domestic supply replaces imports.
- Nagpur facility: "World's largest single-location cartridge plant" — for 155mm production.
- Commercial production: Q4 FY26 start guided.
- Export opportunity: Turkey (NATO ally) facilitates European market access.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26: "New Medium Calibre Ammunition Manufacturing facility inaugurated in Nagpur. Commercial production slated for Q4 FY26."
- WhalesBook analysis: "Nagpur facility recognized as world's largest single-location cartridge plant. Solar poised to address global 155mm shell crisis."
- Q3 FY26 call: "155mm shell production Q4 FY26 start — anticipating sustained demand for next 6–7 years."
- Q4 FY26 expectations: 155mm shells + Pinaka = key Q4 FY26 defence revenue catalysts.

**Time Horizon:** Immediate (Q4 FY26) — 6–7 years of sustained demand.

### Driver 3: International Business — 40% Revenue, 90+ Countries, 9 Manufacturing Nations

**What is the driver?**
Solar is one of the world's largest industrial explosives companies. International business contributes ~40% of total revenue and growing at 35% YoY — fastest growing segment.

**Mechanism**
- 90+ country customer base: Mining, quarrying, infrastructure globally.
- 9 country manufacturing presence: Africa, Southeast Asia, Central Asia, Middle East, Turkey.
- Turkey presence: Manufacturing in Turkey (NATO ally) provides access to European defense contracts.
- Revenue: INR 1,000+ crore in Q3 FY26 alone (+35% YoY).
- Segment trajectory: From ~INR 2,000 crore/year to INR 4,000+ crore/year potential over 3 years.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26: International business INR 1,000+ crore (+35% YoY). Contributes ~40% of total revenue.
- Q2 FY26: "International business hit INR 960 crore — significant milestone."
- 9M FY26: International business growing 35% YoY consistently.
- Management guided: 20% overall annual growth for next 3–5 years.

**Time Horizon:** Structural, ongoing.

### Driver 4: Defence Export Orders — Subsidiary Wins + INR 589 Crore Export Order

**What is the driver?**
Solar's subsidiary (EEL) winning international defence export orders — ammunition exports for military customers across the world. India's defence export push creating new market for Solar's products.

**Mechanism**
- January 2026: INR 589 crore export order for defence products over 2 years.
- November 2025: INR 455 crore export order for supply over 2 years.
- Turkey/NATO channel: Manufacturing in Turkey facilitates NATO-compatible ammunition sales.
- India's goal: USD 5 billion defence exports by FY28 — Solar is a primary beneficiary.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- January 30, 2026: "Solar Industries subsidiary secures INR 589 crore defence export orders."
- November 3, 2025 (ICICI Direct): "INR 455 crore export order; order book estimated at INR 3,650 crore."
- Q3 FY26 press release: Defence segment "significant growth with robust execution and higher contribution."

**Time Horizon:** Active and growing — export orders providing incremental FY26–FY28 revenue.

### Driver 5: Mining and Infrastructure Explosives — Domestic Coal India, SCCL Orders

**What is the driver?**
India's massive coal and infrastructure expansion (INR 11 lakh crore infrastructure budget) directly drives industrial explosives demand. Coal India (world's largest coal miner) is Solar's anchor domestic customer.

**Mechanism**
- Coal India volume growth: India accelerating coal mining to 1.3 billion tonne target — explosives demand growing.
- SCCL (Singareni Collieries): Large Telangana-based coal miner — Solar supplier.
- Road and railway excavation: Bharat Mala, rail expansion creating quarrying demand.
- Order book: Domestic Coal India + SCCL orders part of INR 21,000+ crore total order book.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26: "Expanding order book, notably from Coal India, SCCL and defence sector contracts."
- Domestic explosives business provides stable base; defence is the growth accelerant.

**Time Horizon:** Structural, stable — not a high-growth driver but provides base revenue.

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. **Defence revenue concentration risk**: INR 3,000 crore guidance for FY26 requires massive Q4 ramp (INR 1,374 crore in one quarter vs INR 702 crore in Q3). Q4 execution is critical.
2. **Pinaka delivery timeline**: First Pinaka dispatches Q4 FY26 — any delay → revenue pushes to FY27.
3. **155mm production ramp**: Brand new facility, new product, first production run — scale-up risk.
4. **Raw material costs**: Ammonium nitrate (key explosive input) price volatile. Q3 FY26 RM costs fell to 48.71% of revenue — if they revert, margins contract.
5. **Regulatory risk**: Explosives manufacturing is highly regulated globally. One incident → reputational and regulatory impact.
6. **Valuation risk**: Market cap ~INR 1.25 lakh crore at INR 13,868/share. High P/E reflects growth expectations; any guidance miss → significant valuation compression.
