## 1. IMPORTANT CONTEXT: FY26 TRANSITION YEAR

CSL's FY26 is characterised by PAT decline despite revenue growth — a consequence of the expiry of the high-margin IAC (INS Vikrant — aircraft carrier) project and teething challenges with two newly commissioned facilities (New Dry Dock and ISRF). The structural position is strong: largest order book in CSL's history (INR 21,100 crore as of Q1 FY26), new infrastructure enabling a step-change in capacity, and a pipeline of INR 2.85 lakh crore (13x the order book). FY26 should be seen as a margin reset year before the new-capacity revenue cycle matures in FY27–FY28.

## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: New Dry Dock (310m) — Unlocking Largest Vessel Segments

**What is the driver?**
CSL's new 310-metre stepped Dry Dock (Dock No. 3) is the largest dry dock in India — capable of accommodating SuezMax tankers, Capesize bulk carriers, aircraft carriers, LNG carriers, and offshore jack-up rigs. This is a transformative capacity expansion.

**Mechanism**
- New vessel categories: SuezMax (170,000 DWT), Capesize (180,000 DWT), LNG carriers, VLCCs, jack-up rigs
- Merchant shipbuilding pivot with HD KSOE (South Korea) — leveraging new dock for large commercial vessels
- Repair capability: 310m dock enables repairs of very large vessels previously routed to Singapore/Dubai/China
- Revenue potential: Each SuezMax new build = ~INR 550–700 crore; VLCCs = ~INR 850 crore
- HD KSOE partnership: Technology transfer + collaborative merchant shipbuilding for export markets

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q1 FY26 Investor Presentation: "Both major capital projects — the new Dry Dock and ISRF — are completed and operational."
- Q1 FY26 call: MoUs signed with HD KSOE for merchant shipbuilding. New shipbuilding order book: INR 19,600 crore.
- Q3 FY26: Shipbuilding segment revenue INR 1,013 crore (+56.5% YoY) — large dry dock driving accelerated execution.

**Time Horizon:** Active now and expanding — First large merchant builds under HD KSOE expected FY27.

---

### Driver 2: International Ship Repair Facility (ISRF) — High-Margin International Repair Revenue

**What is the driver?**
CSL's ISRF at Willingdon Island features a 6,000-tonne ship lift with 6 workstations — capable of simultaneously repairing 6 vessels up to 130m. Capacity: 80+ ships per year.

**Mechanism**
- Throughput: 80+ ships/year through ISRF vs 30–40 previously
- International customers via Drydocks World UAE MoU — ship repair clusters in India
- Ship repair margins: 18–24% PBIT vs shipbuilding's 10–12% — every repair vessel is high-value
- US Navy MSRA discussions ongoing for US Pacific Fleet vessel repairs

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q1 FY26: ISRF operational. Drydocks World MoU signed. "Targeting Maersk for ship repair in FY26."
- Q2 FY26: Ship repair revenue INR 360 crore (+27% YoY) — ISRF early contribution
- Q3 FY26: Ship repair INR 337 crore (-32.6% YoY) — project timing issue, not structural. ISRF ramp ongoing.
- CARE Ratings: "Anticipated robust order execution in ship repairs post-ISRF becoming operational."

**Time Horizon:** Medium term (FY27–FY29) — ISRF full utilisation target FY28.

---

### Driver 3: Defence Shipbuilding — Naval Vessels and INR 2.85 Lakh Crore Pipeline

**What is the driver?**
CSL's largest category: INR 13,700 crore defence order book (65% of shipbuilding book). Bidding for INR 10,000 crore additional defence projects in FY26.

**Key Programmes**
1. P17A Stealth Frigates: CSL building 4 of 7 ordered (INR 4,000–5,000 crore each)
2. Next-Generation Corvettes (NGC): CSL expected as one of the builders alongside GRSE
3. Landing Platform Docks (LPD): Large amphibious ships for Indian Navy
4. Coast Guard vessels: Annual OPV and interceptor boat orders
5. IAC-2 (second aircraft carrier): No confirmed timeline, but CSL is the only capable builder

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q1 FY26 Investor Presentation: Defence order book INR 13,700 crore. Pipeline INR 2.85 lakh crore (13x order book).
- Q1 FY26: "Bidding for defence projects worth INR 10,000 crore in FY26. No IAC-2 timeline yet."
- Q3 FY26: Shipbuilding revenue +56.5% YoY driven by P17A execution acceleration.

**Time Horizon:** Multi-year (ongoing) — P17A deliveries through FY29; IAC-2 would extend to FY33+.

---

### Driver 4: Merchant Shipbuilding + Export — HD KSOE and Conoship Netherlands Stake

**What is the driver?**
CSL entering global merchant shipbuilding export market — first time in company history. Two strategic partnerships: HD KSOE (South Korea) and Conoship International Netherlands (23% stake acquired — ship design house with European clients).

**Mechanism**
- HD KSOE: Design + technical expertise + client network for large commercial vessels
- Conoship Netherlands: European commercial clients — tankers, general cargo, ferries, offshore vessels
- Udupi-CSL subsidiary: Already delivered first dry cargo vessel to European client (April 2025)
- Indian shipbuilding costs: ~20–30% lower than South Korean/Japanese yards for similar specs

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q1 FY26: MoU with HD KSOE "for merchant shipbuilding using new dry dock." Udupi-CSL delivered first dry cargo to European client.
- Q3 FY26 (January 30, 2026): Board approved 23% stake in Conoship International Holding B.V. (Netherlands).
- Q1 FY26: Orders for luxury river cruise vessel (Heritage River Journeys) and 70T bollard pull tugs (Polestar Maritime).

**Time Horizon:** Medium term (FY27–FY30) — Partnerships established; first large export contracts expected FY27.

---

### Driver 5: Green Maritime Technologies — HBL JV + IMO 2030 Tailwind

**What is the driver?**
IMO mandates zero-carbon shipping by 2050. CSL forming Green Maritime Propulsion Pvt Ltd JV with HBL Engineering (60% HBL, 40% CSL) to develop marine batteries, BMS, electric motors, and charging infrastructure.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 (January 27, 2026): Board approved JV with HBL for electric mobility and energy storage in maritime.
- March 25, 2026: Green Maritime Propulsion Pvt Ltd incorporated. Initial capital INR 9 crore.
- Q1 FY26: 19th Electric Hybrid Water Metro Boat delivered to Kochi Metro — 4 years of EV vessel experience.

**Time Horizon:** Long term (3–5 years) — JV formation stage; technology development before significant revenue.

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. **IAC margin absence**: PAT -18.5% YoY 9M despite +15.5% revenue growth. New capacity absorbing overhead.
2. **Other income dependency**: Q2 FY26 — other income = 84.9% of PBT (near-zero operational earnings). Q3 improved to 36.2%.
3. **Two stalled passenger ships**: 65% complete, INR 665 crore spent, no delivery. Counterparty dispute ongoing.
4. **Borrowings surge**: INR 459 crore (March 2025) → INR 5,724 crore (September 2025). Interest costs +158% YoY in Q3.
5. **ISRF ramp-up timeline**: Overhead of new facility being absorbed before full utilisation.

Margin recovery and ISRF full utilisation are the key FY27–FY28 catalysts.
