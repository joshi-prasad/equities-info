**Sources: Q4 FY26 earnings call (May 7, 2026); Q3 FY26 official press release (NSE, Jan 30, 2026); Q2 FY26 earnings call (Perivis, Nov 4, 2025); Univest Q4 recap; ScanX FY26 summary; MarketsMojo Q4 analysis.**

---

## 1. COMPANY PROFILE

ACME Solar Holdings Limited is a pure-play IPP developing, owning, and operating solar, wind, and hybrid (solar+wind+BESS) projects under long-term PPAs with SECI, NTPC, NHPC, and SBI. FY26: Revenue INR 2,507 Cr (+59% YoY), PAT INR 498 Cr (+99% YoY), EBITDA INR 2,265 Cr (+61% YoY, ~90% margin). Total portfolio: 8,071 MW including 17 GWh BESS pipeline. India's largest single BESS commissioning: 2.3 GWh (Q4 FY26).

---

## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: Capacity Addition — 450 MW FY26 + 5.1 GW Under Construction

**What is the driver?**
Each commissioned MW generates 25-year recurring PPA revenue. FY26 commissioning: 450 MW including first-ever wind capacity. 5.1 GW under-construction pipeline = the next ~5x revenue step. FY27 guided commissioning: ~1.5 GW (3x FY26 pace).

**Evidence**
- Q4 FY26 (May 7, 2026): "2,990 MW operational. 100 MW wind fully commissioned. 301 MW new FDRE project won with SECI."
- FY26 call: "Under-construction: 5.1 GW. Total portfolio: 8,071 MW including 17 GWh BESS. FY27 target: ~1.5 GW commissioning."

---

### Driver 2: BESS — India's Largest, INR 2.2 Crore/Day Merchant Revenue

**What is the driver?**
2.3 GWh BESS commissioned Q4 FY26 — India's largest. Merchant arbitrage: buys at solar rates (INR 2.5–4.5/unit), dispatches at peak rates (INR 6–14/unit). Currently generating INR 2.2 crore/day. Target: 10 GWh BESS by CY2027.

**Evidence**
- Q4 FY26: "2.3 GWh BESS commissioned. Net BESS revenue: INR 2.2 crore per day."
- Q3 FY26 call: "BESS commissioning upgraded to 2 GWh in Q4 FY26 (from 1 GWh). 2 GWh more in Q1 FY27."
- Q2 FY26: "1 GWh merchant BESS adds ~INR 170 crore annual EBITDA."

---

### Driver 3: Debt Cost Reduction — 150 bps YoY, CRISIL/ICRA AA-

Cost of debt: 10% (FY25) → 8.4% (FY26). Credit upgraded to AA- (CRISIL + ICRA). Cash ROE: 20.6%. 100 bps reduction = ~INR 200 crore/year additional PAT.

---

### Driver 4: FDRE Projects — INR 4.0–5.0/Unit vs INR 2.5/Unit Vanilla Solar

- Q3 FY26: 250 MW NHPC PPA at INR 4.33/unit. Q4 FY26: 301 MW FDRE project won with SECI.
- Project-level IRR target: 18–20% for FDRE vs 14–16% for vanilla solar.

---

### Driver 5: CUF and Operational Excellence

FY26 CUF: 25.9%. Q4 CUF: 26.9%. Plant availability: 99.5%. Grid availability: 99.2%. Q4 generation: 1,720 MUs (+13.7% YoY).

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. Rising interest costs: 5.1 GW under-construction debt scales before revenue commences.
2. Grid connectivity delays: Transmission build-out slower than solar commissioning.
3. FY27 execution: 1.5 GW target = 3x FY26 pace.
4. Tariff compression: New solar auctions at INR 2.4–2.7/unit vs old fleet at INR 3.5–4.5/unit.
5. Merchant BESS regulatory risk: DSM regulations could impact arbitrage revenue.
6. CFO change: Rajat Kumar Singh resigned; Arun Chopra appointed May 8, 2026.
