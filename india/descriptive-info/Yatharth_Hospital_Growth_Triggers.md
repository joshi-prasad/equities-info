## 1. COMPANY PROFILE

Yatharth Hospital & Trauma Care Services Limited operates a chain of **multi-specialty hospitals** concentrated in the Delhi-NCR region and select Tier 2/3 catchments in North India. The group focuses on high-acuity care across specialties like cardiology, neurology, orthopaedics, critical care, and trauma.

As of Q3 FY26:
- Network bed capacity: **~2,550+ beds**, with further beds under expansion.
- Occupancy: **~67% overall**, with some mature hubs (Noida) running above 90%.
- ARPOB (average revenue per occupied bed): **~₹33,700**, up ~10% YoY, indicating pricing power and richer case-mix.
- Newly acquired/greenfield hospitals in **New Delhi, Faridabad Sec-20, and later Agra** are in ramp-up phase and contributing an increasing share of revenues.

## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: Aggressive Capacity Expansion and New Hospitals Ramp-Up

**What is the driver?**  
Yatharth’s growth is led by rapid expansion of bed capacity through acquisitions/new units (New Delhi, Faridabad Sec-20, Agra) and scaling them to mature occupancy and ARPOB levels.

**Mechanism**
- Q3 FY26 revenue: **₹3,205 mn**, up **46% YoY**; new hospitals (New Delhi + Faridabad Sec-20) contributed **₹279 mn**, ~9% of revenue in their first full quarter.
- These new hospitals are fully private-payer/TPA — **0% government scheme dependence**, helping support higher ARPOB and margins once ramped.
- Agra hospital (≈250 beds, ~1.65 lakh sq ft) integration effective from Feb 2026, expected to contribute meaningfully to revenue and EBITDA from day one.

**Why it matters**
- Expansion adds **structural volume growth** independent of same-store growth.
- Private-payer heavy mix improves blended ARPOB and protects margins versus scheme-heavy peers.

---

### Driver 2: Strong Same-Store Growth — Occupancy + ARPOB

**What is the driver?**  
Existing hospitals continue to deliver robust growth through higher occupancy and higher ARPOB.

**Mechanism**
- Q3 FY26: Existing hospitals grew **~33% YoY**; new hospitals added another 9% of revenue.
- Occupancy: overall ~67%; Noida ~91%, Greater Noida ~74%, Noida Extension ~61%, Jhansi Orchha ~72%.
- ARPOB: ~₹33,744 in Q3, up ~10% YoY; Noida Extension ARPOB at ~₹44,000 (+16% YoY).
- Growth driven by mix shift to higher-acuity procedures, better payer mix, and optimised length of stay.

**Why it matters**
- Same-store growth is a key **quality-of-growth marker** in hospitals — high SSG with strong margins indicates underlying demand and execution strength.

---

### Driver 3: Margin Profile — High-20s Adjusted EBITDA on Mature Base

**What is the driver?**  
Yatharth delivers robust profitability with scope for improvement as new units mature.

**Mechanism**
- Q3 FY26 reported EBITDA: **₹742 mn**, up **35% YoY**; reported EBITDA margin: **23.2%** (down ~190 bps YoY due to ramp-up costs in new hospitals).
- Adjusted EBITDA margin (excluding early losses of New Delhi & Faridabad): **~29.2%** — a better reflection of steady-state margins.
- For 9M FY26, revenue: **₹8,577 mn** (+32% YoY); PAT: **₹1,264 mn** (+~38% YoY), highlighting operating leverage.

**Why it matters**
- High-20s adjusted EBITDA margin positions Yatharth among the more profitable hospital platforms; as new units stabilise, reported margins should converge towards adjusted levels.

---

### Driver 4: Case-Mix and Payer-Mix Optimisation

**What is the driver?**  
Management is intentionally skewing towards higher-yield specialties and private/TPA payers.

**Mechanism**
- New Delhi and Faridabad hospitals currently generate **100% revenue from cash/TPA**, with zero government scheme exposure.
- Across the network, the share of high-acuity and complex procedures is rising, lifting ARPOB.
- Strategic focus on specialties with higher realisations (cardiac, neuro, orthopaedics, oncology) and reduced low-yield cases.

**Why it matters**
- A richer case/payer mix directly supports **higher ARPOB and margins**, without necessarily requiring commensurate bed growth.

---

### Driver 5: Balance Sheet and Cash Generation

**What is the driver?**  
Although detailed FY26 balance sheet numbers are pending Q4, interim data points indicate a net cash position and strong operating cash flows.

**Mechanism**
- Q3 FY26 commentary highlights net cash of around ₹200 Cr on the balance sheet even after expansion capex.
- Strong adjusted EBITDA margin in the high-20s and improving scale from new hospitals creates healthy free cash flow as ramp-up stabilises.

**Why it matters**
- Net cash + strong EBITDA gives Yatharth flexibility to continue expansion (brownfield/bolt-on M&A) without stressing leverage.

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. **Execution Risk in New Hospitals:** Ramp-up of New Delhi, Faridabad, and Agra must hit occupancy and ARPOB targets; delays can depress margins and ROCE.
2. **Regulatory/Price Controls:** Any tightening of price caps or new government health schemes with lower realisations could weigh on ARPOB and margins.
3. **Concentration Risk:** High exposure to Delhi-NCR and North India; regional shocks (policy, competition, epidemics) could have outsized impact.
4. **Manpower Cost Inflation:** Hospitals are people-intensive; nurse/doctor wage inflation can pressure margins if not offset by pricing and mix.
5. **Competition:** Established chains (e.g., Apollo, Fortis, Max) are active in similar catchments; competition for doctors and patients can influence pricing and growth.
