## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: Advanced Pharmaceutical Intermediates — Volume + Mix + CDMO Ramp

Pharma intermediates are ~85% of FY26 revenue and carry ~28% segment margins. The growth mechanism operates on three levers:
- **Volume:** Q3 FY26 Pharma revenue ₹351 Cr (+47% YoY); Q4 FY26 +43.9% YoY.
- **Backward integration:** >90% of intermediates integrated with basic chemicals, ensuring cost control.
- **CDMO ramp:** Four additional CDMO products validated, expected to contribute in FY27; each project ₹50–100 Cr revenue potential. Three CDMO projects commenced by Q4 FY26.

### Driver 2: Margin Re-Rating — 23% to 42.4% EBITDA in One Year

FY26 margin expansion is structural:
- **Product mix:** CDMO and complex intermediates carry 2–3x margins of commodity chemicals.
- **Portfolio rationalisation:** Exit from low-margin offerings.
- **Operating leverage:** 5-year sales CAGR 26.84% vs EBIT CAGR 38.56%.
- Gross margin Q3 FY26: **57%** (+1,080 bps YoY).
- H1 FY26 ROCE reached **21.30%** (all-time high).
- **FY27 guidance:** Stable at 34.5–35% EBITDA (management may revise upward).

### Driver 3: Battery Chemicals — Electrolyte Additives Commissioned Q4 FY26

- Jhagadia facility (₹177 Cr capex): 2,000 MT VC + 2,000 MT FEC — **commissioned January 2026**.
- Revenue from electrolyte additives commenced **Q4 FY26**.
- 2,000 MT capacity **fully contracted under signed 3-year customer agreements**.
- Phase 2 capacity expansion completion: **Q1 FY27**.
- Two more battery products to commercial scale in FY27.
- Management: "Meaningful contribution in FY27, ramping quarter-by-quarter."

### Driver 4: Semiconductor Chemicals — India's Only Photoresist Manufacturer

- **Only manufacturer of semiconductor-grade photoresist chemicals in India**.
- **Indichem JV (South Korea):** 75% stake, KRW 30 Bn (~₹190 Cr) invested; R&D centre operational, samples being sent to customers in Japan, South Korea, Taiwan.
- Commercial production: **late CY2026/early CY2027** — running ahead of schedule.
- Policy tailwind: India Semiconductor Mission 2.0 (Budget 2026), Electronics Components outlay ₹40,000 Cr.

### Driver 5: FY27 Growth Guidance — 25%+ Revenue, Stable Margins

- Management guided **25% revenue growth in FY27** at Q4 concall.
- May revise guidance upward later in FY27.
- Three independent verticals by FY28: pharma intermediates, battery chemicals, semiconductors.
- 10x R&D capacity expansion planned (multi-disciplinary facility covering pharma, battery, semiconductor, cosmetics).

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. **Gas supply chain disruption:** Gulf tensions impacting feedstock; working capital days up to 120 from 114.
2. **Battery Chemical ramp:** Phase 2 delay could push FY27 contribution to FY28.
3. **Indichem commercial timeline:** Late CY2026 target; execution risk.
4. **CDMO concentration:** Loss of one large CDMO client = material revenue impact.
5. **42.4% EBITDA sustainability:** Record Q4 margin; whether FY27 can sustain 35%+ requires product mix discipline.
