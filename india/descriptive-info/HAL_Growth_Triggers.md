## 1. IMPORTANT CONTEXT: THE DUALITY OF FY26

HAL's FY26 is defined by a fundamental contradiction:
- **Order book**: INR 2.54 lakh crore as of March 31, 2026 (from INR 1.89 lakh crore at April 1, 2025) — INR 65,000 crore net addition in a single year — most visible 7–8 year revenue pipeline of any Indian defense PSU.
- **Revenue execution**: INR 32,250 crore (provisional) — only 4.1% growth vs FY25's INR 30,981 crore. Well below 8–10% guidance.
- **Root cause of miss**: LCA Mk1A deliveries disrupted by GE F-404 engine supply delays (US export controls) and HTT-40 technical supply chain issues.
- **Narrative**: Pipeline never stronger. Near-term execution constrained by foreign engine supply — a solvable problem. $2.4 billion GE engine deal signed confirms pathway to FY27 ramp.

## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: LCA Mk1A (Tejas) — INR 62,370 Crore New Order + GE Engine Resolution

**What is the driver?**
83+97 = 180 Tejas Mk1A aircraft total (INR 48,000 crore + INR 62,370 crore = INR 1,10,370 crore combined). Largest single program in HAL's order book. GE F-404 engine supply now unlocking deliveries.

**Mechanism**
- $2.4 billion deal with GE for 113 F404-GE-IN20 engines (entire second batch of 97 aircraft)
- Third LCA production line commissioned FY26 — capacity now 24+ aircraft/year vs 12/year
- 5 Tejas aircraft ready and awaiting engines as of Q3 FY26 — immediate deliveries once engines arrive
- Revenue per aircraft: ~INR 540–643 crore (first/second batch rates)
- Revenue potential: INR 6,000–8,000 crore/year at full delivery pace for 7–8 years

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 (February 12, 2026): "Five Tejas aircraft fully ready for delivery to IAF. Awaiting engine supplies from GE Aerospace."
- FY26 provisional: 97 LCA Mk1A (INR 62,370 crore) received; included in INR 2.54 lakh crore order book.
- CLSA (April 2026): "LCA Mk1A fighter deliveries starting in Q1 FY27" as key catalyst. "Clearer GE engine supply timelines" as key watchpoint.
- PL Capital: "Five Tejas aircraft ready for delivery; maintain Buy with INR 5,338 target."

**Time Horizon:** Immediate ramp (FY27 onwards) — 14 aircraft delivered FY26; 24+/year from FY27.

---

### Driver 2: Advanced Light Helicopter (ALH) + Dhruv NG + IMRH Pipeline

**What is the driver?**
Helicopter business accelerated in FY26 to compensate for LCA delays. New orders, new models, and long-term IMRH (111 naval helicopters) provide multi-year pipeline.

**Key Programs**
1. ALH Dhruv: 6 ALH CG (Coast Guard, INR 2,704 crore) — new FY26 order
2. LCH Prachand: India's first indigenous combat helicopter — ramp continuing
3. Dhruv NG: Maiden flight achieved FY26. First civilian order: Pawan Hans (10 aircraft)
4. IMRH: 111 naval helicopters for Indian Navy (~INR 20,000–25,000 crore future programme)
5. Civil MRO: Airbus helicopter maintenance with Safran — growing revenue stream

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- FY26 provisional: "Accelerated deliveries of ALH helicopters supported revenue despite LCA and HTT-40 challenges."
- FY26: Dhruv NG maiden flight. Pawan Hans order for 10 Dhruv NG. H-228 aircraft exported to Guyana.
- New orders: 6 ALH CG (INR 2,704 Cr) + 8 Dornier CG (INR 2,186 Cr) in FY26.

**Time Horizon:** Active and growing — ALH and Dhruv NG delivering FY26–FY29 revenue floor.

---

### Driver 3: Engine Manufacturing — AL31-FP, RD-33, Kaveri, GE-414 Co-Production

**What is the driver?**
Engine manufacturing = strategic pillar + growing revenue share. AL31-FP (Su-30MKI) + RD-33 (MiG-29) ongoing. GE-414 co-production for LCA Mk2/AMCA = transformational future program.

**Key Programs**
1. AL-31FP: Manufactures + overhauls for 262 Su-30MKI fleet — large, recurring revenue
2. RD-33: Overhauls for MiG-29 fleet
3. GE-414 MII: Co-production framework for LCA Mk2 + AMCA — most valuable potential engine program
4. Second HTT-40 production line: Commissioned FY26 (basic trainer)

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- FY26 provisional: "Accelerated deliveries of AL31-FP and RD-33 engines supported revenue."
- CLSA (April 2026): "Progress on the GE 414 MII production deal" as key future catalyst.
- HAL signed MoU with Mishra Dhatu Nigam for strategic metal bank for critical raw materials.

**Time Horizon:** AL31-FP/RD-33: Active, ongoing 10+ years. GE-414 MII: Medium term FY28+.

---

### Driver 4: Repair, Overhaul & Services (ROH) — Recurring High-Margin Business

**What is the driver?**
ROH = HAL's most stable and high-margin business. Maintaining entire Indian armed forces fleet of HAL-built platforms. Base compounds as more platforms are delivered.

**Platform Base**
- Su-30MKI (262), MiG-29 (60+), Hawk (123), Jaguar (124 in service), ALH (300+ delivered), Chetak/Cheetah (200+)
- Civil helicopter MRO (Airbus EC-series) via Safran JV — growing
- ISRO and space segment services

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26: ROH growth guided at 10–15% YoY. Strong execution in ROH vs manufacturing.
- FY26 provisional: "ROH, spares, and related services expected to remain strong contributors."
- Q2 FY26: ROH segment supported EBITDA despite manufacturing margin pressure.

**Time Horizon:** Structural and permanent.

---

### Driver 5: AMCA — Long-Term Wildcard with Short-Term Setback

**What is the driver?**
India's 5th-generation stealth fighter. HAL was expected prime. Key FY26 development: **HAL excluded from AMCA prototype phase** — major strategic setback. However, HAL retains GE-414 engine co-production role.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Aviation Outlook (April 30, 2026): "The unprecedented exclusion of HAL from the AMCA prototype phase is reshaping the aerospace power map."
- HAL retains GE-414 engine role regardless of airframe prime contractor.
- AMCA production: contingent on prototype phase outcome — still possible for HAL.

**Time Horizon:** Very long term (FY30+ for revenue). Prototype exclusion = near-term strategic risk.

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. **LCA engine supply dependency**: GE F-404 deliveries subject to US export policy. FY26 missed guidance due to this. $2.4B deal signed but geopolitical risk persists.
2. **AMCA prototype exclusion**: HAL losing ground in 5th-generation combat aircraft prime contractor role.
3. **EBITDA margin gap**: 9M FY26 at 24.6% vs guidance of 30–31% — product mix and employee cost one-offs.
4. **Employee cost inflation**: +28.5% YoY Q3 FY26 (partly new labour codes one-time; partly structural wage growth).
5. **ALH safety legacy**: Past grounding incidents damage export reputation.
6. **Revenue back-loading**: HAL's Q4 historically = 30–35% of full-year revenue — execution concentrated, creates operational pressure.

The revenue shortfall is attributable to GE F-404 engine supply disruptions — with $2.4B engine deal signed, FY27 should see LCA deliveries normalise to 24+/year. AMCA prototype exclusion requires monitoring as a structural risk to HAL's long-term combat aircraft monopoly.
