## 1. COMPANY PROFILE

Adani Total Gas Limited (NSE: ATGL) is India's leading **City Gas Distribution (CGD)** company, jointly promoted by Adani Group and TotalEnergies (France). It distributes piped natural gas (PNG) to homes and industries, and compressed natural gas (CNG) to vehicles, across PNGRB-authorised Geographical Areas (GAs).

## 2. KEY CONTEXT: GAS COST HEADWINDS — A TEMPORARY MARGIN COMPRESSION

FY26 PAT marginally declined despite +14% volume and +18% revenue, due to:
- APM gas allocation to CNG segment reduced to **~41%** (balance via NWG, HPHT, R-LNG — all costlier).
- Q2 FY26: Gas cost +30% YoY; Q3: Gas cost +18% YoY.
- ATGL's calibrated partial pass-through: prioritised volume retention over full cost recovery.

**Structural relief from FY27:** PNGRB simplified transmission tariff from three-zone to **two-zone** effective January 1, 2026 — domestic PNG and CNG Transport now in Zone-1 (lower cost). Full benefit flows in FY27.

## 3. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: CNG Network Expansion — 705 Stations (+25 in Q4, Highest-Ever Quarter)

- CNG volume growth: Q2 +16%, Q3 +17%, Q4 **+17% YoY** — consistent and accelerating.
- Station additions accelerating: Q2 +12 → Q3 +18 → Q4 **+25** (highest-ever quarterly addition).
- 705 stations at FY26-end vs ~590 at start = **+20% network expansion** in one year.
- Pan-India (with IOAGPL JV): **1,169 CNG stations** (+49 in Q4).
- Each new station typically adds 80–120 kg/day CNG once stabilised.

### Driver 2: PNG Connections — 10.99 Lakh, 49,504 New Homes in Q4 (Highest-Ever Quarter)

- Q2 FY26: Crossed **1 million homes** (10.2 lakh).
- Q3 FY26: 10.5 lakh homes (+34,210 in quarter).
- Q4 FY26: **10.99 lakh** — added **49,504 new homes** (highest-ever quarterly addition).
- Pan-India (with IOAGPL): **13.1 lakh homes** serving 5+ million people.
- 2.5 lakh new PNG connections in FY26 = **~10–15 MMSCM/year** future incremental volume (as consumption ramps over 2–3 years).

### Driver 3: Industrial & Commercial — Highest-Ever Q4 Addition

- Q4 FY26: **9,965 I&C connections** (+214 new in Q4 — improving trend).
- Pan-India: **11,529 I&C connections** (+429 new in Q4).
- I&C customers are highest-EBITDA segment: large volumes, low service cost per unit.
- New GA commissioning: 9 CGS + 1 LCNG plant added in FY26, gasifying new geographies.

### Driver 4: EV Charging — 5,100 Points, New Revenue Stream

- Q2: 4,209 → Q3: 4,908 → Q4: **5,100 EV charging points** (+699 in Q3 alone).
- Co-located at CNG stations; low incremental capex.
- Q4 FY26 concall: Management specifically highlighted EV charging as a strategic growth area.
- Emerging adjacency — not yet material to P&L, but optionality-rich for energy transition.

### Driver 5: FY27 EBITDA Guidance ₹1,500 Cr — +22% YoY Recovery

- Management guided FY27 EBITDA **~₹1,500 Cr** (vs ₹1,225 Cr in FY26 = **+22% YoY** implied).
- Levers: Zone-1 tariff benefit full-year, CNG volume growth, PNG ramp, I&C scaling.
- EBITDA margin recovery expected as gas cost normalises.

## 5. KEY RISKS

1. **APM allocation:** Any further cut to APM gas for CNG = costlier replacement gas = margin pressure.
2. **Gas price volatility:** R-LNG/HPHT spot fluctuations; full pass-through risks volume.
3. **PNG volume ramp lag:** New connections take 1–3 years to reach full consumption.
4. **Competition in GAs:** PNGRB may allocate additional GAs to competitors in ATGL areas.
5. **Adani Group overhang:** Group events can affect financing and sentiment.
