# RR Kabel Ltd — Momentum Investment Snapshot

## Business Summary

RR Kabel is transitioning from a:

* branded retail wire company
  to
* higher-growth institutional cable + export business.

Core earnings engine is now:

* Power & Special Cables
* exports
* operating leverage from Project RRise.

---

# Current Investment Thesis

## Bullish Thesis

* Cable demand remains strong.
* Cable utilization near 90–95%.
* Operating leverage expanded sharply in FY26.
* EBITDA growth (+61.8%) far outpaced revenue growth (+27.6%).
* ROCE improved to ~28%.
* Export business appears structurally strong:

  * high certifications
  * pricing discipline
  * lower commodity risk.
* Project RRise can structurally increase earnings power if absorption succeeds.

---

## Key Risks

Main risk is NOT debt.

Main risk is:

* working capital deterioration
* inventory expansion
* weak cash conversion.

Major warning signs:

* Inventory days: 59 → 81
* CFO/PAT conversion collapsed
* Payables stretched heavily
* FCF nearly zero.

Biggest concern:
Current earnings may partially reflect:

* copper inflation
* channel stocking
* peak utilization
* supplier financing.

---

# Current Business Position

## Positive Signals

* Strong cable volume growth.
* Strong export momentum.
* Margin expansion still continuing.
* ROCE improving.
* Management execution on operating leverage has been strong.
* Project RRise appears strategically necessary.

---

## Negative Signals

* Asset turnover deteriorating.
* Inventory intensity rising sharply.
* Cash flow quality weakened materially.
* Working capital becoming increasingly capital consumptive.
* FMEG turnaround still weak.
* Management commentary becoming somewhat promotional.

---

# What Matters Most Now

## Highest Priority KPIs

### 1. Cable Volume Growth

Most important KPI.

Thresholds:

* > 20% = healthy
* 15–18% = caution
* <15% = thesis weakening.

---

### 2. Inventory Days

Most important balance-sheet signal.

Thresholds:

* <65 = healthy
* ~70 = manageable
* > 80 = dangerous.

---

### 3. CFO / PAT Conversion

Measures earnings quality.

Thresholds:

* > 100% = strong
* 75–100% = healthy
* <60% = weak.

---

### 4. Cable Utilization

Key for margin sustainability.

Thresholds:

* > 85% = strong
* <75% = overcapacity risk rising.

---

### 5. Incremental ROCE

Determines whether Project RRise creates value.

Watch for:

* improving returns on new capital
  vs
* late-cycle overinvestment.

---

# Cycle Interpretation

## Most Likely Current Position

RR Kabel appears:

* late-mid-cycle on existing assets
  but
* attempting a new growth cycle via Project RRise.

Current margins likely reflect:

* peak utilization
* strong infrastructure cycle
* operating leverage benefits.

The next phase depends on:

* successful absorption of new cable capacity.

---

# What Would Strengthen the Thesis

* Cable growth sustains >20%.
* Inventory normalizes toward ~60 days.
* CFO improves materially.
* Export growth remains strong.
* RRise absorption remains healthy.
* Incremental ROCE stays high.

---

# What Would Weaken the Thesis

* Cable growth drops below 15%.
* Inventory remains elevated.
* CFO remains weak.
* Utilization falls.
* Margins peak while cash flow weakens.
* Working capital stress worsens further.

---

# Final Investment View

RR Kabel currently looks like:

* a genuine operating leverage story
  with
* strong structural cable/export opportunities,

BUT

the business is simultaneously showing:

* clear balance-sheet stress
* weakening cash conversion
* signs of possible late-cycle overheating.

The next 2–3 quarters are critical.

Most important question:

> Is Project RRise creating a structurally higher earnings base,
> or
> is FY26 close to peak-cycle profitability?
