# Data Patterns (India) Limited — Growth Triggers Analysis

**Q4 FY26 Status: Not yet declared as of May 10, 2026.**
**Sources: Official earnings call transcripts Q1 (August 7, 2025), Q2 (November 13, 2025), Q3 (February 5–6, 2026); Q3 FY26 official press release and investor presentation; BSE filings at datapatternsindia.com.**

---

## 1. COMPANY PROFILE

Data Patterns (India) Limited is India's most profitable listed pure-play defense electronics company. EBITDA margins: 44–45%. PAT margins: 33–34%. Gross margins: 77%. All products based on proprietary in-house IP — the source of extraordinary margins. Core products: avionics sub-systems, electronic warfare (EW) systems, radar systems, communication systems, weapon guidance electronics. Customers: IAF, Indian Army, Indian Navy, DRDO, HAL, BEL, BrahMos, ECIL.

---

## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: IP-Driven Proprietary Product Portfolio — Structural Margin Moat

**What is the driver?**
100% proprietary IP products. Gross margins 77%, EBITDA 44–45%, PAT 33–34%. Operating leverage compounds these margins as revenue scales.

**Mechanism**
- In-house VLSI design, PCB fabrication, system integration, testing (EMI/EMC facility being set up)
- Qualification moat: Multi-year defense certification process; once qualified, Data Patterns is sole/preferred supplier for program lifetime
- QIP capital deployed: INR 445.89 crore of INR 487.74 crore raised (as of Q3 FY26); INR 41.85 crore remaining for product development + EMI/EMC facility

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 press release: Gross margin 77.44%, EBITDA margin 44.81%, PAT margin 33.67%
- CMD guidance (Q3 call): "Committed to 20–25% revenue growth over medium term, EBITDA margins 35–40%"

**Time Horizon:** Structural and permanent — IP ownership is core competitive advantage.

---

### Driver 2: Electronic Warfare Systems — INR 15,000–20,000 Crore Revenue Potential (2–5 Years)

**What is the driver?**
EW suites for Indian military platforms — among highest-value defense electronics. Management quantified combined EW + radar development potential at INR 15,000–20,000 crore over 2–5 years.

**Key Programs**
1. Modern EW Suite (airborne): Development for IAF fighters/helicopters. Large production + development orders from ECIL and MoD received in Q3 FY26.
2. Electronic Warfare for Naval Platforms
3. Portable EW/Jamming systems for Army

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 earnings call: "Key development areas include the modern EW suite and flight control radars — estimated INR 15,000–20,000 crore revenue potential over 2–5 years."
- Q3 FY26 press release: "Large production and development orders for electronic warfare suites from ECIL and the Ministry of Defence."

**Time Horizon:** Short to Medium term (FY27–FY30).

---

### Driver 3: Radar Systems — Flight Control Radar, AMCA Partnership with Bharat Forge

**What is the driver?**
Developing proprietary flight control radars and airborne fire-control radars — most complex and highest-value avionics systems.

**Key Programs**
1. Flight control radar (in development, part of INR 15,000–20,000 crore combined EW+radar potential)
2. Ground-based surveillance radars (in production)
3. AMCA radar consortium: Partnering with Bharat Forge to bid for AMCA 5th-generation fighter radar

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 earnings call: "Data Patterns engaging with large Indian corporates, notably partnering with Bharat Forge to bid for AMCA production."
- Q1 FY26: "Products of international quality... expected to soon be tested and lead to bigger contracts."

**Time Horizon:** Medium to Long term (FY27–FY31).

---

### Driver 4: Record Order Book — INR 1,868 Crore + INR 1,100 Crore Conversions Imminent

**What is the driver?**
Order book reached all-time high of INR 1,868 crore (December 31, 2025). INR 760 crore confirmed + INR 1,108 crore negotiated/pending release. Additionally, INR 1,100 crore (~INR 11 billion) expected to formally convert within 1–2 months of Q3 call.

**Order Book Trajectory FY26**
- April 1, 2025: INR 730 crore
- Q1 FY26: INR 1,079 crore (+INR 320 crore new orders in Q1)
- Q2 FY26: ~INR 1,300 crore (+INR 351 crore new orders in H1)
- Q3 FY26: INR 1,868 crore (all-time high) — +156% growth in 9 months

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 CMD (S. Rangarajan): "Our order book stands at an all-time high of INR 1,868 crore, providing strong revenue visibility."
- Q3 FY26 call: "Contracts totalling approximately INR 11 billion, previously negotiated but not awarded, expected to convert within 1–2 months."
- Q3 FY26: Working capital cycle improved from 428 days (March 2025) to 340 days (December 2025).
- Export order book: INR 63 crore (Q3 FY26).

**Time Horizon:** Immediate to Medium term (FY26–FY29) — 2.6x FY25 revenue covered by order book.

---

### Driver 5: Sub-System to Full Systems Integrator — TAM Expansion Strategy

**What is the driver?**
Transitioning from sub-system supplier (supplying modules to primes like HAL, BEL, BrahMos) to full systems + solutions provider — prime contractor for complete radar, EW, or communication systems.

**Mechanism**
- Bharat Forge partnership for AMCA: Large-system prime contractor aspiration
- Co-development with global defense majors in radar and EW
- Chennai facility expansion planned
- Revenue target: INR 2,000–2,400 crore medium term (from INR 580 crore 9M FY26 revenue)

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q2 FY26: "Strategy shifting from a subsystem supplier to a full systems and solution provider."
- Q3 FY26: "Co-development activities with global defense majors, particularly in radar and EW domains."
- Q1 FY26: Revenue target INR 2,000–2,400 crore; INR 2,000–3,000 crore orders expected FY26.

**Time Horizon:** Medium term (FY27–FY30).

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. **Revenue lumpiness**: Q2 distorted by single large low-margin contract. Revenue swings quarter-to-quarter.
2. **Working capital intensity**: 428 days → 340 days (improving). Still high. Cash recharge expected Q4 FY26.
3. **Customer concentration**: BrahMos, ECIL, HAL, MoD form concentrated base.
4. **Development contract milestone timing**: Revenue recognition milestone-dependent; delays possible.
5. **Capacity constraint**: Facility expansion in planning — execution of INR 2,000+ crore target requires physical expansion.
6. **Competition from BEL**: BEL retains PSU advantage in large EW/radar system bids.
