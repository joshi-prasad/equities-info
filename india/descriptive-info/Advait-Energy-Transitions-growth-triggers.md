# Advait Energy Transitions (ADVAIT)

### Growth Triggers

**PTS Division — Discom EPC and transmission tooling as revenue flywheel.** The Power Transmission Solutions (PTS) division is the dominant growth engine, contributing ~84% of the order book. The discom EPC business alone grew ~46% in 9M FY26. The division manufactures and installs products like ACSS/ACSR conductors, OPW, ERS (Energy Recovery Systems), and high-temperature low-sag conductors — niche products that are predominantly imported and qualify for Make-in-India tailwinds. The ₹216 crore PGVCL order (largest in company history) began revenue recognition in Q4 FY26.[^3][^4][^5]

**NRE Division — Solar EPC, BESS, Green Hydrogen as structural optionality.** The New and Renewable Energy division, while only 16% of the order book today, is the long-term pivot. The company completed its first solar EPC project (KP Green, 30 MW) and a 12.5 MW block of the 100 MW Adani order during Q3–Q4. BESS is being developed for a 2.5 GWh factory targeting commissioning by Q2 FY27. An electrolyzer assembly unit (30 MW Phase 1) was targeted for completion by March 15, 2026, with a 300 MW expansion roadmap and ₹90–100 crore fundraising at the subsidiary (Advait Green Energy).[^5][^3]

**NSE Main Board Listing.** In January 2026, Advait listed on NSE Main Board, expanding its investor base and improving capital access.[^5]

### Revenue and Margin Evolution

9M FY26 consolidated revenue grew **138% YoY to ₹486 crore**; Q3 FY26 consolidated revenue grew 114% YoY to ₹211 crore. On a standalone basis, Q3 revenue was ₹124.7 crore (+32% YoY), with EBITDA at ₹21.1 crore (margin: **16.92%**) and PAT at ₹12.5 crore (margin: **10.03%**). Consolidated EBITDA margin in Q3 was **11.45%** — lower than the standalone figure because the NRE division is still in capability-building mode and operating below sustainable margin levels.[^6][^3][^5]

The margin compression at the consolidated level is intentional: management explicitly stated they are sacrificing near-term consolidated margins to build EPC qualifications in solar, BESS, and green hydrogen. Once these businesses reach critical mass (targeted by FY28), consolidated margins are expected to converge with standalone levels.[^5]
