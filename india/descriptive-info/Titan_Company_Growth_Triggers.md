# Titan Company Limited — Growth Triggers Analysis
**Sector:** Lifestyle Retail — Jewellery (Tanishq, CaratLane, Mia, Zoya, Damas), Watches, Eyecare, TEAL, Emerging
**FY26 Status: Q4 & Full Year DECLARED — Results announced May 8, 2026**
**Sources: Titan Company official FY26 press release (titancompany.in, May 8, 2026); Univest Q4 FY26 analysis; ScanX FY26 full-year analysis; Chola Securities Q4 PAT; Multibagg Q4 FY26 breakdown; ICICI Direct FY26 result summary; MarketsMojo Q4 FY26 margin analysis.**

---

## 1. COMPANY PROFILE

Titan Company Limited is India's largest lifestyle brand company. Products: Jewellery (Tanishq, Mia, Zoya, CaratLane, Damas — GCC), Watches (Titan, Fastrack, Sonata, EDGE, Helios), Eyecare (Titan Eye Plus), TEAL (Titan Engineering & Automation — defence/aerospace), Emerging (SKINN, Taneira, IRTH). FY26: Consolidated total income INR 76,078 Cr (+33% YoY), PAT INR 5,073 Cr (+52% YoY). Q4 FY26: Total income INR 20,300 Cr (+46%), PAT INR 1,179 Cr (+35%). Dividend: INR 15/share.

---

## 2. IMPORTANT CONTEXT: FY26 AS LANDMARK YEAR

Titan crossed INR 50,000 Cr revenue in FY25 (after 40 years). FY26 added another INR 25,000 Cr in a single year — reaching INR 76,078 Cr. MD Ajoy Chawla: "The next INR 25,000 crore has been remarkably achieved in a single year of FY26."

---

## 3. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: Tanishq/India Jewellery — Exchange Programs, Festival of Diamonds, Wedding Demand

India Jewellery Q4 FY26: Revenue INR 17,114 Cr (+46%). EBIT INR 1,902 Cr at 11.1% margin. Tanishq + Mia + Zoya: INR 16,047 Cr (+48%). Exchange programs: low customer acquisition cost, high loyalty. Festival of Diamonds campaign: powered 35% growth in both gold and studded portfolios. High single-digit buyer growth in Q4 despite elevated gold prices (INR 90,000+/10g).

**Evidence**
- Titan official (May 8): "Tanishq, Mia, Zoya combined INR 16,047 Cr (+48%). Festival of Diamonds powered 35% growth in gold and studded."
- ICICI Direct: "India Jewellery EBIT INR 1,902 Cr at 11.1% margin."

---

### Driver 2: CaratLane — Digital-Native Jewellery, Youth Segment, 22% Growth

CaratLane Q4 FY26: Revenue INR 1,066 Cr (+22%). EBIT INR 89 Cr at 8.4% margin. Digital discovery → physical purchase model. 5 CaratLane stores added Q4 alone.

---

### Driver 3: Damas Acquisition — 123 GCC Stores, International Jewellery Platform

67% of Damas Jewellery LLC acquired January 2026 for INR 2,858 Cr total (INR 1,190 Cr for 67%). 123 GCC stores across UAE, Saudi, Oman, Kuwait, Bahrain, Qatar. International Jewellery Q4: INR 1,081 Cr (+174% with Damas). Integration phase: INR -82 Cr EBIT Q4 (integration costs). Call option for remaining 33% (deadline December 2029).

**Evidence**
- Titan official: "Completed 67% acquisition of Damas for INR 2,858 Cr. 4 new Tanishq stores in GCC. 123 stores added."
- Univest: "International Jewellery grew 174% to INR 1,081 Cr."

---

### Driver 4: TEAL — Defence/Aerospace, 60% Growth, 17.9% EBIT Margin (Highest in Portfolio)

TEAL Q4 FY26: Revenue INR 454 Cr (+60% YoY). EBIT INR 81 Cr at 17.9% margin — highest in Titan's portfolio. Segments: Aerostructures, precision components (defence/space), automation solutions. India defence capex growing 15–18%/year.

**Evidence**
- Titan official: "TEAL 60% YoY growth. Total income INR 454 Cr. Automation solutions continued order accretion."
- ScanX: "TEAL EBIT INR 81 Cr at 17.9% margin."

---

### Driver 5: Watches Premiumisation — Analog Growth, 11.7% EBIT Margin

Watches Q4: Revenue INR 1,222 Cr (+8%). EBIT margin 11.7%. Analog watches: double-digit consumer sales growth in volume + price. Fastrack refresh targeting youth.

## 5. KEY RISKS

1. **EBITDA margin gap**: 9.5% actual vs 11–11.5% guidance. Damas integration losses + retail expansion compressing margins.
2. **Gold price risk**: INR 90,000+/10g compressing jewellery EBIT margins.
3. **Damas integration**: Currently loss-making (INR -82 Cr Q4). GCC competitive landscape differs from India.
4. **Q4 PAT decline QoQ**: PAT fell 30% sequentially (INR 1,684 Cr Q3 → INR 1,179 Cr Q4).
5. **Valuation premium**: EV/EBITDA 51.45x, EV/EBIT 56.90x — any miss = sharp de-rating.
6. **Employee costs**: +28% QoQ in Q4 on rapid retail expansion.
