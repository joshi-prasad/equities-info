### One important observation

* **Nitin Spinners** is a **future capacity story**—its major earnings acceleration depends on commissioning and ramping up new facilities.
* **PGIL** is a **capacity utilisation story**—it already has infrastructure that can absorb incremental orders with limited additional investment.
* **Arvind** is a **business mix story**—earnings improve if garmenting and AMD continue to outgrow legacy fabrics.
* **KPR** is an **execution story**—it is already operating efficiently, so growth depends on continued garment expansion and export demand rather than unlocking idle capacity.

For a **6–12 month UK FTA rerating thesis**, this specific comparison suggests **PGIL appears to have the strongest immediate operating leverage**, while **Arvind and KPR** are positioned to benefit through execution and product mix. **Nitin's larger upside is likely to be realized after its expansion comes online rather than immediately.**
