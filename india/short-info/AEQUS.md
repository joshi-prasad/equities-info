# AEQUS Ltd. – Investment Summary

## Company Snapshot

AEQUS is evolving from a precision contract manufacturer into a **capability acquisition and industrialization platform** serving global OEMs across Aerospace and Consumer industries.

Unlike conventional engineering companies, AEQUS does not compete through proprietary product design or IP.

Instead, the company competes through:

* OEM qualifications
* vertically integrated manufacturing ecosystems
* manufacturing process know-how
* customer proximity
* capability acquisition
* wallet-share expansion
* supply-chain consolidation

Its business today operates through two very different economic engines:

| Business | Economic Role |
|----------|---------------|
| Aerospace | Cash generation + high ROCE + capability platform |
| Consumer Electronics & Toys | Operating leverage + future earnings engine |

The company should not be viewed simply as an aerospace contract manufacturer.

Instead, AEQUS is becoming:

**A capability-driven manufacturing platform that acquires complex manufacturing know-how, industrializes it inside India's vertically integrated ecosystems, expands wallet share with existing OEMs, and monetizes those capabilities through operating leverage and improving ROCE.**

---

# Core Investment Thesis

The investment thesis is **NOT** higher aircraft production.

Nor is it simply about outsourcing to India.

The current opportunity is driven by:

* Consumer Electronics utilization ramp
* operating leverage from already deployed capital
* aerospace wallet-share expansion
* migration toward higher-value components
* vertical integration
* increasing manufacturing content per aircraft
* improving capital allocation after IPO deleveraging

Over the last several years AEQUS invested heavily in:

* manufacturing ecosystems
* consumer electronics capacity
* workforce
* aerospace qualifications
* vertical integration
* global capability acquisition
* customer relationships

The business is now transitioning from:

**Capability Creation Phase → Commercialization & Operating Leverage Phase**

The key question is no longer:

**"Can AEQUS win customers?"**

The key question is:

**"Can AEQUS monetize the manufacturing capabilities and capacity it has already built by increasing utilization, expanding wallet share and improving ROCE?"**

# Business Model Summary

## 1. Aerospace (Current Cash Engine)

The historical foundation of the business.

Products include:

* Aerostructures
* Engine components
* Landing gear components
* Actuation systems
* Hydraulic assemblies
* Aircraft wheels

Major customers include:

* Airbus
* Boeing
* Safran
* Collins Aerospace

Key advantages:

* Long qualification cycles
* High switching costs
* D2P status with Airbus
* Long-term OEM contracts
* Integrated manufacturing ecosystem

This business generates:

* strong EBITDA margins (~24%)
* healthy ROCE
* stable cash generation

It finances the expansion of the newer businesses.

---

## 2. Consumer Electronics (Operating Leverage Engine)

The most important earnings catalyst over the next 2–4 quarters.

Products include:

* Portable computer enclosures
* Precision machined electronics components

Key characteristics:

* Massive installed capacity
* Only ~23% utilization
* Significant fixed-cost absorption
* Commercial production has commenced

Unlike Aerospace, most of the investment has already been made.

The investment thesis now depends on:

**Utilization rather than further capacity expansion.**

Every incremental order should carry disproportionately high incremental margins because depreciation, facilities and manpower have already been absorbed.

---

## 3. Toys & Consumer Durables

Produces for:

* Mattel
* Wonderchef
* Tramontina

This business is strategically important because it utilizes existing molding capacity.

The key objective is replacing Hasbro volumes with Mattel while improving utilization of the Koppal manufacturing ecosystem.

This is primarily a utilization story rather than a structural value creator.

---

## 4. Capability Acquisition Platform (The Hidden Business Model)

This is arguably the company's biggest strategic advantage.

AEQUS systematically acquires manufacturing capabilities through:

* overseas acquisitions
* global partnerships
* joint ventures
* customer proximity

Capabilities are then:

**Acquired → Qualified → Industrialized → Scaled inside India**

Examples include:

* France (SIRA) for engine and landing gear capabilities
* SQuAD JV for aerospace forging
* API JV for surface treatment
* Consumer electronics leveraging aerospace machining capability

The company increasingly creates value by commercializing acquired manufacturing capabilities rather than developing proprietary products.

# Why The Moat Exists

The moat is not precision machining.

The moat is:

**Qualification + Vertical Integration + Manufacturing Ecosystems + Capability Transfer + Customer Trust**

Key evidence:

* D2P status with Airbus
* 15+ year customer relationships
* Integrated aerospace SEZ
* In-house forging
* In-house surface treatment
* Complex aerospace qualifications
* Ability to consolidate fragmented global supply chains

The strongest competitive advantage is:

**Its ability to continuously increase manufacturing content and wallet share inside existing OEM programs.**

Factories can be replicated.

Integrated ecosystems, qualifications and customer trust cannot.

# Financial Inflection

FY26 appears to represent the company's largest operating leverage inflection.

Major developments include:

* IPO-driven deleveraging
* Net Debt / Equity reduced to ~0.1x
* Consumer capacity fully commissioned
* Commercial shipments commenced
* Aerospace order book expanded to approximately $889 million
* Aerospace margins remained strong
* Consumer losses largely reflect idle capacity

Management has already incurred:

* capex
* depreciation
* workforce hiring
* manufacturing infrastructure

The next phase depends on:

**Capacity Utilization → Operating Leverage → Margin Expansion → ROCE Improvement**

rather than

**Capacity Expansion → Revenue Growth**

The company has effectively entered its "asset sweating" phase.

# Final Investment View

AEQUS appears to be transitioning from:

* a precision contract manufacturer,
* qualification-heavy engineering company,
* capacity-building business

toward

* a capability acquisition platform,
* vertically integrated manufacturing ecosystem,
* operating-leverage story,
* wallet-share compounder.

The Aerospace business continues to generate:

* cash flow
* high margins
* strong ROCE
* customer relationships
* manufacturing credibility

The next phase of value creation will likely come from:

* Consumer Electronics utilization
* operating leverage
* increasing wallet share
* higher-value landing gear and engine components
* improving capital efficiency
* better cash generation

Importantly, AEQUS is no longer growing primarily by adding customers.

It is increasingly growing by:

* acquiring manufacturing capabilities,
* expanding manufacturing content inside existing customers,
* industrializing those capabilities inside India's integrated ecosystems,
* and improving productivity of assets that have already been built.

The key question is no longer:

**"Can AEQUS manufacture aerospace components?"**

The key question is:

**"Can AEQUS efficiently monetize its integrated manufacturing ecosystems by increasing utilization, expanding wallet share and improving ROCE?"**

If management succeeds, FY27–FY29 could see meaningful improvement in:

* Earnings quality
* EBITDA margins
* ROCE
* Free cash flow
* Capital efficiency
* Business mix
* Operating leverage

The single most important variable to monitor is:

**Consumer Electronics Capacity Utilization.**

Aerospace protects the downside through stable cash generation and wallet-share expansion, while Consumer Electronics provides the upside through operating leverage. The investment thesis succeeds only if the consumer business absorbs the fixed-cost base that has already been created and converts AEQUS from a capacity-building company into a high-return manufacturing platform.
