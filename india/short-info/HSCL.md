# HSCL – Investment Summary

## Company Snapshot

Himadri Speciality Chemical (HSCL) is transitioning from an integrated carbon chemistry company into an advanced battery materials platform.

The legacy business operates across:

- Coal Tar Pitch
- Carbon Black
- Specialty Carbon Black
- Coal Tar Derivatives

These businesses generate strong cash flows through backward integration, captive power generation, and specialty carbon chemistry.

Management is now deploying this cash flow to build higher-value battery material businesses including:

- Synthetic Graphite Anodes
- Silicon-Carbon (SiCx) Anodes
- LFP Cathode Materials

The company should **not** be viewed as a commodity chemical manufacturer.

Instead, HSCL is becoming:

**An integrated carbon chemistry platform attempting to move up the battery materials value chain through its existing carbon-processing capabilities.**

---

# Core Investment Thesis

The investment thesis is **NOT** higher coal tar prices or carbon black demand.

The current opportunity is driven by:

- commercialization of battery anode materials
- expansion into specialty carbon products
- structural margin advantages from backward integration
- captive power generation
- improving product mix
- leveraging decades of carbon chemistry expertise

Over the last several years HSCL has invested heavily in:

- specialty carbon capabilities
- battery material R&D
- anode manufacturing
- strategic investments (Sicona, IBC, Invati)
- battery supply-chain positioning

The business is now transitioning from:

**Carbon Chemistry → Advanced Battery Materials**

The key question is:

**Can HSCL successfully commercialize battery anode materials while preserving the cash-generating economics of its legacy carbon chemistry platform?**

# Business Model Summary

## 1. Coal Tar & Pitch (Legacy Cash Engine)

The historical foundation of the business.

Products include:

- Coal Tar Pitch
- Distillation products
- Specialty oils

Advantages:

- Backward integration
- Long customer relationships
- Strong export presence
- Stable cash generation

This business funds future growth investments.

---

## 2. Carbon Black & Specialty Carbon Black (Current Earnings Engine)

The highest-quality business today.

Products include:

- Tire-grade Carbon Black
- Specialty Carbon Black
- Conductive Carbon Black

Advantages:

- Higher margins
- Better pricing power
- Internal feedstock integration
- Operating leverage through captive power

Management continues shifting product mix toward specialty grades.

---

## 3. Battery Anode Materials (Future Growth Engine)

The most important long-term opportunity.

Products include:

- Synthetic Graphite
- Natural Graphite
- Hybrid Anodes
- Silicon-Carbon (SiCx) Anodes

Strategic advantages:

- Proprietary anode-grade coal tar pitch
- In-house carbon chemistry
- Sicona technology partnership
- Commenced commercial anode production

Unlike many battery startups, HSCL already controls a significant portion of the upstream carbon value chain.

This appears to be the strongest strategic fit for the company.

---

## 4. LFP Cathode Materials (Long-Term Optionality)

HSCL is developing:

- Lithium Iron Phosphate (LFP) Cathode Materials

The ambition is significant, but commercial execution remains early.

Compared with anodes, LFP requires:

- new supply chains
- lithium sourcing
- phosphate sourcing
- new manufacturing capabilities

Therefore, LFP currently represents **optionality rather than the core investment thesis.**

---

## 5. Birla Tyres (Capital Allocation Wildcard)

The acquisition represents a move into:

- Consumer tyres
- B2C branding

While management views this as a long-term diversification opportunity, it remains outside HSCL's core competency in carbon chemistry.

Future value depends on disciplined capital allocation rather than aggressive expansion.

The moat is **not** coal tar processing.

The moat is:

**Backward Integration + Carbon Chemistry Know-how + Proprietary Feedstock + Process Integration**

Key evidence:

- Anode-grade coal tar pitch
- Decades of carbon chemistry expertise
- Internal feedstock production
- Captive power generation
- Integrated manufacturing
- Strategic battery technology partnerships

The strongest competitive advantage lies in:

**Converting internally produced carbon feedstock into higher-value battery materials.**

Few companies possess this combination of integration and technical capability.

# Final Investment View

HSCL appears to be transitioning from:

- an integrated carbon chemistry company

toward

- an advanced battery materials platform built upon its existing carbon-processing capabilities.

The legacy business continues to generate strong cash flows through:

- Coal Tar Pitch
- Specialty Carbon Black
- Carbon Chemistry
- Captive Power
- Backward Integration

The next phase of value creation will likely come from:

- Battery Anode Materials
- Silicon-Carbon (SiCx)
- Higher specialty product mix
- Better capital allocation
- Commercial battery-material revenues

Importantly, the investment case should not be viewed as a broad EV battery story.

The strongest strategic fit lies in battery **anodes**, where HSCL already possesses adjacent capabilities, proprietary feedstock, and technical know-how.

The key question is no longer:

**"Can HSCL grow its legacy carbon chemistry business?"**

The key question is:

**"Can HSCL successfully commercialize battery anode materials while preserving the cash-generating economics of its legacy carbon chemistry platform?"**

If management succeeds, FY27-FY30 could see meaningful improvement in:

- Earnings quality
- Business mix
- ROCE
- Cash generation
- Capital efficiency
- Valuation multiple

The single most important variable to monitor is:

**Commercial Scale-up and Customer Adoption of Battery Anode Materials.**
