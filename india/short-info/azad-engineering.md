# Azad Engineering Ltd. – Investment Summary

## Company Snapshot

Azad Engineering is a precision manufacturing platform supplying highly complex, mission-critical components for:

* Gas turbines
* Steam turbines
* Nuclear turbines
* Aircraft engines

The company serves global OEMs including:

* Mitsubishi Heavy Industries (MHI)
* Siemens Energy
* Baker Hughes
* GE Vernova
* Boeing
* Airbus
* Rolls-Royce
* Pratt & Whitney
* Safran

Unlike conventional engineering companies, Azad competes primarily through:

* Qualification capability
* Process engineering
* Manufacturing reliability

rather than product design.

The company is best viewed as a:

**Qualification-driven precision manufacturing platform.**

---

# Core Investment Thesis

The investment thesis is NOT new customer acquisition.

The current opportunity is driven by:

* monetization of past qualifications,
* ramp-up of newly built dedicated facilities,
* wallet-share expansion within existing customers,
* operating leverage,
* and recovery in capital efficiency.

Over the last several years Azad has invested heavily in:

* qualifications,
* customer relationships,
* manufacturing infrastructure,
* dedicated facilities,
* and workforce expansion.

FY26 appears to have been the infrastructure and stabilization year.

FY27-FY28 should determine whether these investments can translate into:

* higher throughput,
* stronger cash flows,
* improving ROCE,
* and sustained earnings growth.

# Business Model Summary

## 1. Energy Segment (Current Earnings Engine)

Contributes ~80%+ of revenue.

Products include:

* Gas turbine airfoils
* Combustion components
* Steam turbine components
* Nuclear turbine components

This remains the primary earnings engine.

Major customers include:

* MHI
* Siemens Energy
* GE Vernova
* Baker Hughes

Key driver:

Global demand for reliable baseload power, including AI and data-center related power infrastructure.

## 2. Aerospace & Defence (Growth Engine)

Supplies:

* Aircraft engine components
* Landing gear systems
* Defence-related precision components

Key programs include:

* Boeing
* Airbus
* Rolls-Royce
* Pratt & Whitney
* Safran

This business carries:

* higher qualification barriers,
* stronger customer stickiness,
* and potentially higher long-term margins.

---

## 3. Dedicated Facility Model

This is the most important strategic development.

Azad has established dedicated facilities for:

* MHI
* Siemens
* GE
* Baker Hughes

These facilities effectively integrate Azad into customer supply chains and significantly increase switching costs.

The company is increasingly evolving from:

Supplier → Strategic Manufacturing Partner

---

# Why The Moat Exists

The moat is not machinery.

The moat is:

**Qualification + Process Engineering + OEM Integration**

Key evidence:

* ~1,700 qualified parts
* 45+ qualified manufacturing processes
* 30–48 month qualification cycle per component
* 10–15 year customer relationships
* Dedicated customer facilities
* Single-source supplier status in selected programs (MHI hot-section nozzle vanes)

The longer qualification cycles become, the stronger the moat becomes.

# Financial Inflection

FY26 appears to be a transition year.

The company has already incurred:

* major capex,
* workforce investments,
* depreciation,
* and working-capital buildup.

The next phase should focus on:

* sweating assets,
* increasing throughput,
* improving asset turns,
* and generating operating cash flow.

Management's long-term framework implies:

Capacity Build → Utilization Ramp → Asset Turn Expansion → ROCE Recovery

The success of the investment thesis depends far more on utilization than on additional capacity creation.

# Bull / Base / Bear Framework

## Bull Case

* Facility utilization ramps smoothly
* Revenue compounds at 25%+
* Asset turns move toward 1.7x–2.0x
* Inventory normalizes
* Operating cash flow inflects sharply
* ROCE recovers above 20%

## Base Case

* Growth remains around guidance levels
* Utilization improves gradually
* Working capital remains elevated but stable
* ROCE improves slowly
* Cash flow improvement remains gradual

## Bear Case

* Inventory remains elevated
* OEM audits delay throughput
* Asset turns remain weak
* Cash conversion disappoints
* ROCE remains stuck near current levels
* Growth consumes capital without creating value

# Final Investment View

Azad Engineering appears to be transitioning from:

* a qualification-heavy,
* capital-consuming,
* infrastructure-building company

toward:

* a throughput-driven,
* operating-leverage,
* cash-generating precision manufacturing platform.

The key question is no longer:

"Can Azad win customers?"

The key question is:

"Can Azad efficiently monetize the qualifications, capacity and customer relationships it has already built?"

If management succeeds, FY27-FY29 could see significant improvement in:

* asset turns,
* ROCE,
* cash generation,
* and earnings growth.

The single most important variable to monitor is:

**Dedicated Facility Throughput.**
