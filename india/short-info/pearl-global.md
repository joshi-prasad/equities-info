# Pearl Global Industries Ltd. – Investment Summary

## Company Snapshot

Pearl Global Industries (PGIL) is a global apparel manufacturing and supply-chain platform serving leading international fashion retailers and brands.

Unlike traditional garment exporters, PGIL should not be viewed as a low-cost apparel manufacturer.

Instead, the company operates as a:

**Multi-country sourcing and manufacturing platform that helps global brands optimize cost, tariffs, lead time and supply-chain risk.**

Its competitive advantage comes from operating manufacturing facilities across:

- Bangladesh
- Vietnam
- India
- Indonesia
- Guatemala

allowing customers to shift production whenever tariffs, labor costs or geopolitical conditions change.

The business currently derives most of its earnings from:

- Bangladesh (volume engine)
- Vietnam (profit engine)

while simultaneously investing in:

- India recovery,
- Bangladesh expansion,
- Indonesia turnaround,
- Vietnam capacity,
- and backward integration

to create the next growth phase.

---

# Core Investment Thesis

The investment thesis is **NOT** higher garment prices.

PGIL operates in a highly competitive, price-taking industry.

The current opportunity is driven by:

- India margin recovery after tariff normalization
- UK & EU FTAs increasing India's competitiveness
- Better utilization of existing capacity
- Bangladesh expansion and laundry integration
- Vietnam premium product mix
- Higher wallet share from existing global customers

Over the last several years PGIL invested heavily in:

- global manufacturing footprint
- customer relationships
- design capability
- new factories
- supply-chain infrastructure

The business has now transitioned from:

**Capacity Creation Phase → Utilization & Operating Leverage Phase**

The key question is no longer:

**"Can PGIL win customers?"**

The key question is:

**"Can PGIL efficiently utilize its global manufacturing footprint and convert higher volumes into earnings and ROCE?"**

# Business Model Summary

## 1. Bangladesh (Current Volume Engine)

Largest manufacturing base.

Products include:

- Denim
- Knits
- Wovens

Advantages:

- Lowest manufacturing cost
- Large scale
- Duty-free EU/UK access
- Consistently high utilization

This remains the company's largest earnings contributor.

---

## 2. Vietnam (Current Profit Engine)

Focuses on:

- Jackets
- Outerwear
- Performance wear

Advantages:

- Highest realization
- Highest ROCE
- Strong FTAs
- Superior manufacturing capability

Vietnam generates the highest economic returns inside the group.

---

## 3. India (Largest Growth Opportunity)

Historically impacted by:

- US tariffs
- Low utilization
- Margin pressure

However:

- US tariff normalization
- UK FTA
- Expected EU FTA

create significant operating leverage.

India possesses large unused capacity that can generate disproportionate earnings growth with limited incremental capital.

---

## 4. Indonesia (Turnaround Business)

Produces:

- Activewear
- Professional wear

Current issues:

- Under-utilization
- Recent factory relocation

Future value depends primarily on utilization improving toward optimal levels.

---

## 5. Guatemala (Strategic Nearshore Facility)

Small operation serving:

- Fast replenishment for US retailers

Strategically valuable because of:

- short transit times
- customer relationships

Economically it currently contributes little and remains loss-making.

---

## 6. Design & Supply Chain Platform

PGIL increasingly creates value through:

- design support
- sourcing
- production allocation
- logistics
- compliance

rather than simple garment stitching.

The business is increasingly evolving from:

**Garment Manufacturer → Global Supply-Chain Partner**

# Why The Moat Exists

The moat is not garment manufacturing.

The moat is:

**Multi-country manufacturing + Supply-chain orchestration + Customer integration**

Key evidence:

- Manufacturing presence across five countries
- Global design capability
- Long-standing relationships with marquee retailers
- Multi-country production allocation
- Proven execution during tariff disruptions
- Strong compliance infrastructure

The most important competitive advantage is:

**The ability to shift production across countries while maintaining customer relationships.**

Factories can be replicated.

Global supply-chain flexibility cannot.

# Financial Inflection

FY26 appears to be a transition year.

The company has already invested in:

- new manufacturing facilities
- Bangladesh expansion
- India capacity
- Vietnam expansion
- Indonesia relocation

The next phase should focus on:

- sweating existing assets
- improving utilization
- expanding margins
- increasing cash generation
- improving ROCE

Management's long-term framework implies:

**Capacity Build → Utilization Recovery → Operating Leverage → Earnings Growth**

The success of the investment thesis depends far more on utilization than additional capacity creation.

# Bull / Base / Bear Framework

## Bull Case

- India rapidly fills unused capacity
- Bangladesh expansion ramps smoothly
- Vietnam continues premium mix shift
- EBITDA sustainably exceeds 10%
- ROCE returns above 30%
- Revenue crosses ₹6,000 Cr

---

## Base Case

- India recovery remains gradual
- Bangladesh executes as planned
- Vietnam remains strong
- EBITDA stays around 9.5–10%
- ROCE remains around current levels
- Revenue grows in low double digits

---

## Bear Case

- Global apparel demand weakens
- New tariff disruptions emerge
- Bangladesh loses competitiveness
- India recovery stalls
- Indonesia remains under-utilized
- EBITDA remains below 9%
- Capital deployment outpaces earnings

# Final Investment View

Pearl Global appears to be transitioning from:

- a diversified apparel manufacturer,
- capacity expansion story,
- tariff management business

toward:

- a global supply-chain platform,
- operating leverage story,
- capital-efficient earnings compounder.

The key question is no longer:

**"Can PGIL manufacture garments?"**

The key question is:

**"Can PGIL maximize utilization of its global manufacturing network while maintaining customer relationships and improving returns on capital?"**

If management succeeds, FY27-FY29 could see meaningful improvement in:

- earnings growth,
- ROCE,
- operating leverage,
- free cash flow,
- and capital efficiency.

The single most important variable to monitor is:

**India Capacity Utilization.**

If India successfully scales toward its installed capacity following tariff normalization and FTAs, it can become the largest incremental contributor to group earnings without requiring significant additional capital.
