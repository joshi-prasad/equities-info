# TD Power Systems Ltd. – Investment Summary

## Company Snapshot

TD Power Systems (TDPS) is a manufacturer of AC generators and industrial motors supplying global turbine and engine OEMs.

Its generators are used in:

- Gas engines
- Gas turbines
- Steam turbines
- Hydro turbines
- Synchronous condensers

Key customers include global prime mover OEMs such as:

- INNIO
- Siemens Energy
- Baker Hughes
- Voith
- Other global engine and turbine manufacturers

The company should be viewed as:

**A qualification-driven export industrial benefiting from a global power infrastructure cycle.**

Unlike traditional capital goods companies, TDPS does not depend primarily on domestic capex. Its fortunes are increasingly tied to global power demand, particularly AI-driven data center power requirements and grid stabilization investments.

---

# Core Investment Thesis

The investment thesis is NOT domestic power demand.

The current opportunity is driven by:

- Explosive order inflows from global OEMs
- AI and data-center related power infrastructure
- Grid stabilization investments
- Market-share gains due to competitor capacity shortages
- Operating leverage from recently added capacity
- Expansion into larger generator categories

Over the last several years TDPS invested in:

- Plant 3 capacity
- OEM relationships
- Product qualifications
- Export infrastructure
- Engineering capabilities

The business has now moved from:

**Demand Creation Phase → Execution Phase**

Demand visibility is no longer the primary issue.

The key question is:

**Can TDPS convert a rapidly growing order book into revenue, cash flow and returns without execution bottlenecks?**

---

# Business Model Summary

## 1. Generator Business (Current Earnings Engine)

The dominant business and primary profit driver.

Products include:

- Gas turbine generators
- Gas engine generators
- Steam turbine generators
- Hydro generators
- Synchronous condenser generators

Key demand drivers:

- AI data centers
- Distributed power generation
- Grid stabilization
- Industrial captive power

This segment generates most of the company's growth and profitability.

---

## 2. Motors Business (Secondary Growth Driver)

Manufactures:

- Synchronous motors
- Induction motors
- Traction motors

Management has deliberately avoided low-margin commodity motor markets.

The business remains strategically relevant but has recently been deprioritized as generator demand has surged.

Near-term value creation is expected to come primarily from generators rather than motors.

---

## 3. Hydro & Refurbishment

An increasingly attractive niche.

Opportunities include:

- Hydro generator refurbishment
- Replacement projects
- Life-extension programs

This segment provides diversification beyond data-center related demand and may carry attractive margins.

---

## 4. Large Generator Expansion (Future Growth Layer)

The company is investing in capabilities for larger generators and rotor manufacturing.

This is strategically important because it allows TDPS to move into categories historically dominated by global majors.

If executed successfully, this can expand:

- Addressable market
- Customer wallet share
- Long-term earnings power

# Why The Moat Exists

The moat is not manufacturing capacity alone.

The moat is:

**Qualification + OEM Relationships + Cost Advantage + Execution**

Key evidence:

- Long-standing relationships with global OEMs
- Multi-year vendor qualification cycles
- Proven delivery record
- Significant export presence
- 20–30% cost advantage versus many Western competitors
- Capacity availability when competitors are constrained

The most important asset is:

**Approved supplier status with global OEMs.**

Factories can be replicated.

Trust and qualification history cannot.

**Order Book → Execution → Cash Conversion**

# Bull / Base / Bear Framework

## Bull Case

- AI-related power demand remains strong
- OEMs continue expanding capacity
- Quarterly order inflows remain elevated
- Large generator investments succeed
- Working capital stabilizes
- ROCE remains above 30%

## Base Case

- Export demand remains healthy
- Growth moderates from current levels
- Execution remains manageable
- Working capital remains elevated but controlled
- ROCE settles in the high-20s

## Bear Case

- AI infrastructure spending slows
- Global power projects are delayed
- Competitors add capacity
- Logistics disruptions recur
- Working capital expands further
- ROCE compresses materially

# Final Investment View

TD Power Systems appears to be transitioning from:

- A niche generator manufacturer

toward

- A strategically important global power equipment supplier benefiting from structural power infrastructure demand.

The key positive development is that demand visibility has improved dramatically through:

- AI-related power investments
- Gas-engine deployment
- Grid stabilization spending
- Global OEM capacity expansion

Importantly, the current growth is being driven by:

- Real order inflows
- Export demand
- Capacity utilization
- Operating leverage

rather than speculative future opportunities.

The key question is no longer:

**"Can TDPS win orders?"**

The key question is:

**"Can TDPS execute, deliver and convert the backlog into cash while preserving returns?"**

If management succeeds, the next phase could see continued growth in:

- Earnings
- Cash generation
- Export revenues
- Long-term market share

The single most important variable to monitor is:

**Order Inflow Conversion into Revenue and Cash Flow.**
