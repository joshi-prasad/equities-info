# Shilpa Medicare Ltd. – Investment Summary

## Company Snapshot

Shilpa Medicare is transitioning from a traditional oncology API manufacturer into a differentiated specialty pharma platform focused on:

* Complex formulations (FDF)
* Novel Drug Delivery Systems (NDDS)
* Specialty CDMO partnerships
* Peptides and biologics (early stage optionality)

The company historically operated as a low-asset-turn, capex-heavy oncology API business. However, FY26 appears to mark the beginning of a monetization phase where previously built capacities are finally getting utilized.

The current business architecture can be viewed as:

| Segment            | Current Role                 | Strategic Importance              |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------- | --------------------------------- |
| APIs               | Legacy funding engine        | Stable cash generation            |
| FDF (Formulations) | Current earnings engine      | Margin & ROCE expansion           |
| NDDS               | Emerging specialty layer     | High-margin growth                |
| Specialty CDMO     | Future annuity-like business | Sticky commercialization revenues |
| Biologics / GLP-1  | Long-duration optionality    | Narrative optionality only        |

---

# Core Investment Thesis

The central thesis is NOT biologics or GLP-1.

The current rerating appears driven by:

* rapid scaling of the formulation business,
* improving utilization of previously underutilized assets,
* operating leverage,
* NDDS commercialization,
* and early monetization of specialty CDMO capabilities.

The company spent multiple years building:

* manufacturing infrastructure,
* regulatory capabilities,
* peptide/biologics capacities,
* NDDS facilities,
* and specialty formulation platforms.

FY26 is the first year where:

* commercialization is visibly scaling,
* operating leverage is becoming meaningful,
* and cash generation is improving materially.

Key evidence:

* EBITDA margin expanded to ~29%
* Operating cash flow increased sharply
* Incremental ROCE improved materially
* Working capital cycle improved significantly
* CWIP reduced sharply, indicating transition from capex phase to utilization phase

# Business Model Summary

## 1. APIs (Legacy Business)

Historically the core business.

The company manufactures oncology and specialty APIs used either:

* internally for formulations,
* or externally for third-party pharma companies.

Economics:

* Moderate margins
* Competitive market
* Lower pricing power
* High working capital intensity

Strategic role today:

* provides manufacturing scale,
* backward integration,
* and supports formulation margins through captive consumption.

---

## 2. Formulations (FDF) – Current Earnings Engine

The most important business today.

Shilpa converts APIs into differentiated finished dosage forms:

* injectables,
* tablets,
* oncology products,
* specialty therapies,
* oral films,
* and complex generics.

Key products/drivers:

* Europe oncology formulations
* Nilotinib
* NorUDCA (Noduca)
* Pemetrexed
* Bortezomib

Why this matters:

* higher margins,
* better pricing power,
* stronger operating leverage,
* and superior ROCE versus APIs.

The ex-licensing base business grew strongly in FY26, indicating real commercial volume expansion rather than milestone-driven accounting profits.

---

## 3. NDDS (Novel Drug Delivery Systems)

Potentially the highest-quality business inside Shilpa.

Focus areas:

* Transdermal patches
* Oral dissolving films (ODF)
* Long-acting injectables
* Specialty delivery systems

Key programs:

* Rotigotine transdermal patch
* Ondansetron ER (OERIS)
* Tadalafil ODF

Strategic importance:

* lower clinical risk versus biologics,
* higher manufacturing complexity,
* limited competition,
* faster commercialization cycles,
* potentially very attractive ROCE profile.

The company appears to be shifting from:
“molecule competition”
toward:
“delivery-system competition.”

This is strategically important.

---

## 4. Specialty CDMO

Emerging high-quality business.

Shilpa increasingly acts as:

* development partner,
* manufacturing partner,
* and commercialization partner

for biotech and specialty pharma companies.

Key economics:

* sticky customer relationships,
* high switching costs,
* regulatory integration,
* long-duration supply agreements.

Major trigger:

* Unicycive OLC commercialization.

This business may eventually structurally improve:

* earnings quality,
* visibility,
* and margins.

---

## 5. Biologics / GLP-1 / Peptides

Still largely optionality.

Includes:

* Recombinant Human Albumin (rHA)
* GLP-1 peptides
* ADC initiatives
* fermentation platforms

Current status:

* capital intensive,
* delayed monetization,
* uncertain timelines.

These businesses currently consume capital but contribute little to near-term earnings.

Investors should assign limited near-term economic value.

# Bull / Base / Bear Framework

## Bull Case

* Europe formulations continue scaling rapidly
* Rotigotine commercializes successfully
* Unicycive OLC receives approval
* NDDS gains commercial traction
* Utilization expands sharply
* ROCE moves sustainably above 20%

## Base Case

* FDF remains strong
* NDDS scales gradually
* CDMO commercializes selectively
* Biologics remain delayed
* Steady improvement in margins and ROCE

## Bear Case

* Unicycive delayed/rejected
* Biologics continue consuming capital
* Utilization disappoints
* Licensing income weakens
* Depreciation pressure rises
* ROCE expansion stalls

# Final Investment View

Shilpa Medicare increasingly appears to be transitioning from:

* a low-return oncology API business

toward:

* a higher-quality specialty formulation + NDDS + CDMO platform.

The key positive development is that this transition is now becoming visible in:

* margins,
* cash flows,
* ROCE,
* utilization,
* and commercialization metrics.

Importantly, the current monetization appears driven primarily by:

* formulations,
* NDDS,
* operating leverage,
* and specialty commercialization,

rather than speculative biologics optionality.

The next phase of the story will likely depend on:

* successful commercialization execution,
* sustained FDF growth,
* NDDS monetization,
* and disciplined capital allocation.
