## 1. IMPORTANT CONTEXT: FY26 SUMMARY

BEL delivered its strongest-ever full year performance in FY26. Provisional FY26 turnover: INR 26,750 crore (+16% YoY vs INR 23,024 crore in FY25). Order inflows for FY26: INR 30,000 crore (including export orders worth USD 346 million). BEL's FY26 is a record year on every metric.

## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: Radar Systems — Air Defence, Surveillance, Fire Control

**What is the driver?**
BEL is India's premier radar designer and manufacturer. Multi-function phased-array radars, ground-based air defence radars, naval radars, fire control radars, and airborne radars are BEL's highest-value products with expanding order pipeline.

**Key Programs**
1. Arudhra Medium Power Radar: Large orders executed in FY26 for Indian Air Force.
2. Mountain Radars: New order in FY26 for high-altitude terrain surveillance (Himalayas).
3. Air Defence Radars: For Akash missile battery integration.
4. QRSAM (Quick Reaction Surface-to-Air Missile) radar sub-systems: Large upcoming order.
5. Long Range Detection Radar (LRDR): Upcoming programme.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- FY26 turnover announcement (April 1, 2026): "Mountain Radars" listed as major FY26 order won.
- Q3 FY26 call (January 28, 2026): "QRSAM — major upcoming order expected order placement Q4 FY26."
- Q3 FY26: "BEL's strong backlog and backend-loaded deliveries provide comfort on medium-term revenue visibility."
- 9M FY26: LRSAM project execution mentioned as a major driver of INR 5,000+ crore Q3 revenue.

**Time Horizon:** Structural, ongoing — India's radar modernisation is a 10+ year programme.

---

### Driver 2: Electronic Warfare (EW) Systems — BEL's Fastest Growing Segment

**What is the driver?**
EW suite is the fastest growing segment for BEL. India is equipping all IAF fighters, naval vessels, and Army platforms with indigenous EW systems — replacing Russian and foreign systems. BEL is prime integrator for most IAF and Navy EW programmes.

**Key Programs**
1. Shakti EW System: Executed INR 5,000+ crore in Q3 FY26 (mentioned as one of top 7 programmes).
2. EW suite for Helicopters: New FY26 order.
3. EW systems for Naval platforms: New FY26 order.
4. HIMSHAKTI EW system: Mountain warfare EW system — executed in FY26.
5. LCA Mk1A EW sub-systems: Digital LRUs for Tejas fighter.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- FY26 announcement: "EW suite for Helicopters, EW systems for Naval platforms" as major FY26 orders received.
- Q3 FY26 call: "Shakti EW System, HimShakti, LRUs for LCA Mk1A, Shakti EW — these 7 projects accounted for INR 5,000+ crore Q3 revenue."
- Management: EW and radar are the fastest-growing segments.

**Time Horizon:** Growing rapidly — India's entire platform fleet needs EW upgrades (10–15 year programme).

---

### Driver 3: Akash Missile System — Weapon System Electronics

**What is the driver?**
BEL is the prime weapon system integrator for Akash surface-to-air missile system (Indian Army + Air Force + exports). This is BEL's single largest programme by revenue historically.

**Key Programs**
1. Akash Army: New regiment orders — ongoing large programme.
2. Akash 1S: Upgraded variant with seekers.
3. Akash NG (Next Generation): In development with DRDO. Higher capability, higher value.
4. Akash export: Armenia has ordered Akash. More countries in pipeline.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 call: "Akash Army" listed as one of 7 major FY26 projects (INR 5,000+ crore combined).
- Management: "QRSAM order placement likely Q4 FY26 — massive order for BEL."
- BDL Q3 call: BEL + BDL collaboration on Akash NG development.

**Time Horizon:** Active and growing — Akash Army expansion + export + NG variant.

---

### Driver 4: Battlefield Surveillance + Networking + C4I Systems

**What is the driver?**
BEL supplies networked battlefield electronics: C4I systems, SDRs, military communication terminals, battlefield management systems (BMS), counter-UAV systems. India's military digital transformation is a multi-decade programme.

**Key Programs**
1. Battlefield Surveillance Systems: Executed in FY26 as one of top 7 programmes.
2. Lynx Fire Control Solution: Naval fire control — executed Q3 FY26.
3. Mobile Communication Terminal: FY26 order won.
4. Counter-Unmanned Aerial System (C-UAS): New domain — FY26 order.
5. BMS (Battlefield Management System): Army's full digitisation.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 call: "Battlefield Surveillance System and Lynx Fire Control Solution" in top 7 projects.
- FY26 order announcement: "Network systems, Counter UAV System, Mobile Communication Terminals" — new orders.

**Time Horizon:** Growing — Army + Navy digital transformation is 15+ year programme.

---

### Driver 5: Non-Defence Diversification + Export Growth

**What is the driver?**
BEL expanding into non-defence segments (metro systems, defence-adjacent civilian electronics, solar power) and defence exports. FY26 export orders: USD 346 million — record.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- FY26 announcement: "Export orders worth USD 346 million in FY26; export order book USD 495 million."
- Major non-defence: Metro rail electronics, airport systems, EVM (Electronic Voting Machines), industrial electronics.
- EOIR payloads, air defence radars in export pipeline.

**Time Horizon:** Growing — export orders accelerating.

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. **Order execution backend-loading**: Q4 typically 30–35% of full-year BEL revenue. Concentration in Q4 creates delivery risk.
2. **Margin mix sensitivity**: 29% EBITDA in 9M exceeds 27% guidance. Q4 mix of lower-margin products could bring FY26 margin down to guidance level.
3. **QRSAM delay**: If QRSAM order not placed in Q4 FY26, slides to FY27 — large programme.
4. **HAL/BEL inter-dependency**: BEL supplies avionics sub-systems for Tejas, Su-30MKI. HAL production ramp is precondition for some BEL revenue.
5. **AMCA avionics uncertainty**: If HAL is excluded from AMCA, BEL's avionics role also gets complicated.
6. **Labour/Navratna constraints**: PSU wage structure limiting ability to add capacity as fast as private sector.
