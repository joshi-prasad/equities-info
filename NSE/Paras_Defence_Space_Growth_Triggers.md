## 1. COMPANY PROFILE

Paras Defence and Space Technologies is India's only listed pure-play defense optics and space technology company. Four segments: (1) Optics & Optronics Systems (night vision, periscopes, electro-optical, space telescope mirrors), (2) Defence Engineering (heavy engineering structures, pressure vessels, EMC chambers), (3) Defence Electronics (PCBs, EMI/EMP protection), (4) Space Technology (components for ISRO, space telescopes). Revenue: INR 413 crore (FY25 trailing per Screener); PAT: INR 71 crore. 5-year revenue CAGR: 21%.

## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: Optics & Optronics — Defence Night Vision, Thermal Imagers, Space Optics

**What is the driver?**
Paras is India's only domestic manufacturer of high-performance defence optics (thermal imagers, night vision devices, gunner sights, periscopes, space telescope mirrors) — a critical import-substitution play as India pushes toward Atmanirbhar Bharat in sensitive optics technology.

**Mechanism**
- Night vision devices: Army's massive replacement programme for obsolete night vision gear
- Thermal imagers: For tanks (Arjun, T-90), infantry vehicles, naval systems
- Space telescope mirrors: Large optics for ISRO satellites (RISAT, Resourcesat) — unique capability in India
- Ground-to-space optical telescopes: For space domain awareness and satellite tracking
- Gunner Sights: For main battle tanks and combat vehicles
- Laser rangefinders and target designators: For aircraft and ground systems

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 (standalone): Optics/Optronics INR 58.4 crore (+52% QoQ) — strongest segment in Q3.
- Q2 FY26: Optics INR 38.4 crore (flat YoY — Q2 is typically engineering-heavy).
- Q3 FY26 Whalesbook: "Optics/Optronics standout performer: 30% YoY revenue increase to INR 5,840 lakhs."
- DRDO order (post-Q3): "Paras Defence secured ~INR 80.28 crore order from DRDO for air defence optical system." — new high-value single order.
- Q4 FY26 preview (Univest): "Paras is India's leading manufacturer of defence optics; government push for indigenisation directly benefits Paras."

**Time Horizon:** Structural, ongoing — optics indigenisation is a 10+ year mandate.

### Driver 2: Defence Engineering — Expanding Revenue Base + 40% YoY Leap in Q2

**What is the driver?**
Heavy engineering (pressure vessels, EMC/NEMP shielding chambers, fabricated defence structures) — key enabler for weapons test ranges, nuclear submarines, warships, and land systems.

**Mechanism**
- EMI/EMP Protection: Shielded rooms and Faraday cages for military electronics facilities. India's only commercial provider of military-grade EMP protection chambers.
- Structural components: Fabricated steel structures for defence facilities, ships.
- Pressure vessels: For submarine programmes, defence research.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q2 FY26: "Defence Engineering revenue +40.72% YoY to INR 67.28 crore" — strongest ever engineering quarter.
- Q3 FY26: Engineering contributed INR 42.43 crore (standalone) — strong execution.
- 9M FY26: Defence Engineering growing strongly YoY; optics recovering.
- Long-term: EMC/EMP shielded facility market in India growing as military electronics spending rises.

**Time Horizon:** Ongoing — engineering orders complementary to optics.

---

### Driver 3: Paras Avionics Pvt Ltd — New Avionics Subsidiary (FY26 Formation)

**What is the driver?**
In Q3 FY26, Paras's board approved incorporation of a new wholly-owned subsidiary: **Paras Avionics Pvt Ltd** — targeting avionics and aerospace platform applications. This is a strategic expansion from optics → avionics.

**Mechanism**
- Avionics market in India: Currently dominated by HAL, BEL, foreign OEMs. Large import replacement opportunity.
- Paras's adjacency: Defence electronics + optics + space = natural springboard to avionics.
- Target products: Inertial navigation sub-systems, digital map displays, flight management interfaces.
- Revenue potential: Avionics is a INR 5,000–10,000 crore addressable market in India over 5 years.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26 (January 22, 2026): "Board approves incorporation of Paras Avionics Pvt Ltd for avionics/aerospace focus."
- DRDO air defence optical order (post-Q3): Part of integrated air defence systems where avionics + optics converge.
- CNBC-TV18 (Amit Mahajan, Director, January 26, 2026): "FY26 revenue guidance revised to INR 450–500 crore; strong Q4 expected."

**Time Horizon:** Long term (FY28–FY30) — subsidiary formation stage; revenue from FY28+.

---

### Driver 4: Space Technology — ISRO Contracts + Commercial Space

**What is the driver?**
Paras is one of very few private Indian companies producing large optical elements (telescope mirrors, space cameras) for ISRO satellites. As India's space program accelerates (100+ launches planned by 2030), Paras's optics content per satellite grows.

**Mechanism**
- Space telescope mirrors (50cm–2m class): For earth observation, spy satellites, science missions
- Commercial satellite boom: Global commercial satellite orders (Amazon Kuiper, OneWeb, Geosynchronous missions) create demand
- Space domain awareness: Ground-based telescopes for India's space surveillance network
- ISRO Gaganyaan: Crew module components, optical instruments

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- CNBC-TV18 (January 2026): "Q4 expected to bring strong inflows, ending FY26 with INR 1,100–1,200 crore order book."
- Q4 FY26 preview (Univest): "Space segment orders growing. ISRO program expansion — emerging segment with long-term potential."
- FY24 Annual Report: "Order book doubled to INR 630 crore (from INR 393 crore in FY23)."

**Time Horizon:** Medium to Long term (FY27–FY30) — space orders lumpy but growing.

---

### Driver 5: Defence Exports — Emerging International Market

**What is the driver?**
Government push to achieve USD 5 billion defence exports by 2025. Paras's optics products (thermal imagers, night vision) have international demand — Southeast Asia, Middle East, Africa markets. Export orders provide TAM expansion beyond Indian armed forces.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q4 preview: "Any export order announcement would significantly re-rate growth expectations."
- Company exploring exports for optics/optronics to friendly nations.

**Time Horizon:** Emerging — first large export order would be a significant re-rating event.

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. **Small scale**: INR 100–115 crore/quarter revenue. Any order slippage materially impacts quarterly performance.
2. **Revenue lumpiness**: Q1 FY26 = INR 93 crore; Q3 FY26 = INR 106 crore — limited sequential growth. Management targets strong Q4 to hit annual guidance.
3. **Margin compression in Q3**: EBITDA margin 24.65% (down 343 bps QoQ from 28.08%) — ESOP/gratuity charges + input cost mix.
4. **Optics vs Engineering mix**: Engineering delivers higher revenue but optics is higher-margin. Q2 engineering-heavy quarters may see margin dips.
5. **Q4 FY26 results pending**: Expected May 2026 — FY26 guidance requires strong Q4 (INR 145–195 crore needed).
6. **Paras Avionics execution**: Avionics is a more competitive, capital-intensive market than optics. Risk of under-executing on this strategic pivot.
