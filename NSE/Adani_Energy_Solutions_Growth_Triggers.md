## 1. COMPANY PROFILE

Adani Energy Solutions Limited (NSE: ADANIENSOL) is **India's largest private power transmission and distribution company**. Four segments:
1. **Transmission:** 27,949 CKm network; 35-year regulated tariff concessions.
2. **Distribution:** AEML — Mumbai, serving 13 million consumers.
3. **Smart Metering:** 2.46 Cr meter order book (₹29,519 Cr revenue potential); 1 crore+ installed.
4. **Emerging:** C&I power (1,300 MW), District Cooling (88,000 TR under implementation).

## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: Transmission Pipeline — ₹71,779–77,787 Cr Under Construction

AESL's primary growth comes from commissioned transmission assets earning regulated tariff revenue.
- **FY26 commissioning:** 9 transmission projects, including **5 in Q4 FY26 alone**.
- Landmark: **Mumbai HVDC project** — world's first compact 1,000 MW HVDC link; AESL is India's only private player with 2 HVDC completions.
- Under-construction pipeline (Q4): **₹71,779 Cr**.
- Near-term tendering pipeline (next 12 months): **~₹1 lakh crore**.
- System availability consistently **>99.7%**, generating incentive income.

Each ₹10,000 Cr commissioned adds ~₹700–800 Cr annualised regulated revenue (35-year concessions).

### Driver 2: Smart Metering — 1 Crore Milestone, ₹29,519 Cr Order Book

- Q4 FY26: Crossed **1 crore cumulative installations** (milestone achieved ahead of guidance).
- Order book: **2.46 crore meters**, ₹29,519 Cr revenue potential.
- Total addressable market: **103 million meters** yet to be bid nationally.
- Installation run-rate target: 30,000 meters/day.
- Each crore meters = ~₹12,000 Cr of revenue + multi-year O&M income.

### Driver 3: Distribution — RAB +10%, Navi Mumbai/Mundra Licenses

- AEML RAB: **₹10,521 Cr** (+10% YoY in FY26).
- T&D losses: industry-best **4.03%**; collection efficiency >100%.
- **Navi Mumbai + Mundra distribution licenses** pending regulatory approval — ₹10,000 Cr capex planned for Navi Mumbai over 5 years.

### Driver 4: C&I and Emerging — 1,300 MW, District Cooling, Data Centres

- C&I load scaled from 700 MW (Dec 2025) to **1,300 MW** (Jan 2026) — near-doubling in one quarter.
- District Cooling: India's largest facility (40,000 TR) at Mundra under progress; 88,000 TR total under implementation.
- Higher margins than regulated transmission/distribution.

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. **Regulatory/tariff risk:** CERC/SERC tariff orders can impair revenue; approval delays defer commissioning.
2. **Execution:** ₹71,779 Cr pipeline faces land, ROW, and regulatory risks (Jamnagar, Navinal noted Q3).
3. **Smart meter state payment delays:** Discoms slow to pay; revenue recognition timing risk.
4. **Leverage:** Capex at ₹14,232 Cr; leverage at 4–4.5x target.
5. **Adani Group overhang:** Group-level events affect financing costs.
