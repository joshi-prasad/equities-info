# Aether Industries (AETHER)

### Growth Triggers

**Contract/Exclusive Manufacturing (CEM) — Milliken contract and Baker Hughes ramp as twin engines.** The most material FY26 trigger was the 10-year exclusive CEM agreement with Milliken & Company (material science/polymer sector), signed in Q1 FY26. Aether will be the world's sole commercial manufacturer of a new Milliken product, with Site 3+ (dedicated entirely to this contract) completing construction and commissioning by Q4 FY26. Baker Hughes (Site 4, oil & gas) scaled revenues to ₹60 crore in Q3 FY26 from ₹25 crore in Q4 FY25 — a 2.4x jump in two quarters. CEM's share of total revenue rose from ~37% (Q1 FY26) to ~43% (Q3 FY26), indicating a structural mix improvement.[^8][^9]

**Site 5 (Panoli) — First-time-in-India molecules with premium pricing.** The two Phase 1 production blocks at Site 5 (Panoli, Surat) completed construction and water/solvent trials by Q3 FY26. Commercial production is expected from Q4 FY26 onward. Three LSM products (pharma/agro/material science) priced between $30–$40/kg are ready for launch — all made for the first time in India. In addition, Aether entered a semiconductor electronic chemicals CEM contract with a Japanese/Korean/Taiwanese customer base from Site 5 in Q3.[^9]

**European multinational CEM contract.** In Q3 FY26, Aether disclosed a new CEM exclusive contract with "one of the largest chemical companies in Europe" targeting material science — currently a small ticket but expected to scale significantly within 12 months. This is separate from the Milliken and Baker Hughes contracts.[^9]

**R&D expansion as pipeline multiplier.** Lab count is being expanded from 8 to 23 (an additional 15 labs) and fume hoods from 65 to 195 (short and long-term expansion). This directly supports Aether's CRAMS-to-CEM conversion pipeline, which management described as having 12–18 projects in advanced discussion stages with finalization timelines compressed from 18 months to 4–6 months due to global China+1 urgency.[^8][^9]

### Revenue and Margin Evolution

| Period | Revenue | YoY | EBITDA Margin | PAT Margin |
|--------|---------|-----|---------------|-----------|
| Q1 FY26 | ₹258.7 crore | +35% | 30% | 18%[^8] |
| Q2 FY26 | ₹280.1 crore | +30% | ~30% | ~19%[^10] |
| Q3 FY26 | ₹317.1 crore | +44% | 34% | 20%[^9] |
| 9M FY26 | ₹853.4 crore | +43% | ~32% | ~19%[^9] |

The margin expansion is driven by three concurrent forces: (1) higher CEM share (CEM contracts carry similar or better margins than LSM per management guidance); (2) stabilization of Baker Hughes at a higher run-rate, improving Site 4 utilization from zero to ~49% as of Q3; and (3) operating leverage on the R&D and overhead base as total revenue scales 35–44% YoY while headcount growth is controlled.[^8][^9]

Insurance claims (from a prior site incident) are expected to fully settle by end of FY26, releasing any residual P&L drag.[^8]
