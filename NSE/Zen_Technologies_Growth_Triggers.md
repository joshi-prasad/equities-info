---

## 1. COMPANY PROFILE

Zen Technologies Limited is India's leading indigenous defence training simulation and anti-drone systems company. Founded by CMD Ashok Atluri. Products: Tank simulators, artillery simulators, combat training nodes (CTN), anti-drone systems, integrated air defence simulators (IADCS). Customers: Indian Army, Air Force, Navy, NDRF, police. Net cash: INR 1,103 crore (September 2025) — zero debt. FY26 was a year of revenue decline due to delayed government procurement, but FY26 order inflows of INR 431 crore in Q4 alone signal sharp FY27 recovery.

---

## 2. IMPORTANT CONTEXT: FY26 REVENUE DECLINE

FY26 full year: Revenue INR 773 crore (-25% YoY vs FY25's INR 1,032 crore). EBITDA INR 333 crore (-23% YoY). PAT INR 193 crore (-31% YoY). Root cause: **Delayed government procurement** — simulator orders worth INR 650 crore that should have been placed in H1 FY26 were delayed to H2/FY27. EBITDA margin FY26: 48.37% — expanded from 44.36% in FY25 (more evidence that the issue is revenue timing, not profitability). Order book at FY26-end: INR 1,336 crore — a record high. Q4 FY26 order inflows: INR 431 crore alone.

## 3. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: Training Simulators — India's Military Modernisation Mandate

**What is the driver?**
Zen is India's sole large-scale indigenous manufacturer of military training simulators. The Indian military operates the world's largest land forces with ageing training infrastructure. Simulator replacement and expansion is a decades-long programme.

**Key Programs**
1. Tank simulators: T-90, Arjun MBT virtual crew trainers.
2. Artillery simulators: Pinaka, Bofors, K-9 Vajra fire control trainers.
3. Combat Training Node (CTN): New INR 102 crore order Q3 FY26 — company's in-house R&D development.
4. Naval simulation: Via Anawave Systems (76% stake acquired Q3 FY26) — tactical trainers, submarine simulators.
5. IADCS (Integrated Air Defence Combat Simulator): Completed ahead of schedule in Q3 FY26.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26: "Secured INR 102 crore order for Combat Training Node (CTN)."
- Q3 FY26: "Successfully completed IADCS project ~9 months ahead of contractual timeline."
- Q2 FY26: "Simulator orders worth INR 650 crore expected to materialise in H2 FY26."
- FY26 order book (March 2026): INR 1,336 crore — record high.
- Q4 FY26 order inflows: INR 431 crore alone.

**Time Horizon:** Structural, long-term — India's 3,000+ tank fleet, 1,200+ artillery guns, all need simulators.

---

### Driver 2: Anti-Drone Systems — Post-Operation Sindoor Demand Surge

**What is the driver?**
Zen manufactures anti-drone (counter-UAV / C-UAV) systems — the fastest growing new defence segment globally. Post-Operation Sindoor, Indian military demand for anti-drone systems accelerated sharply.

**Mechanism**
- INR 245 crore anti-drone upgrade order (Q3 FY26): Largest single anti-drone order in Zen's history.
- Demand driver: Post-Operation Sindoor + Ukraine war learnings — every military unit needs anti-drone capability.
- Zen's product: Soft-kill + hard-kill integrated anti-drone systems. RF jamming, laser, kinetic interception.
- Management guidance (Q2 FY26): "Expecting roughly 50-50 split between simulators and anti-drone systems in future revenue mix."
- Export: Strong demand from Middle East, EU, Africa, Southeast Asia.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26: "Secured INR 245 crore order for upgradation of Anti-Drone Systems."
- Q3 FY26 Multibagg: "Reinforcing the critical importance of indigenous defence technologies. Enables faster adaptation to evolving threats at significantly lower lifecycle costs."
- Q2 FY26: "Anti-drone systems seeing strong demand post-Operation Sindoor."

**Time Horizon:** Active and rapidly growing — largest single new product category.

---

### Driver 3: Naval Simulation (Anawave Acquisition) — New Platform Expansion

**What is the driver?**
Zen acquired 76% stake in Anawave Systems and Solutions Pvt Ltd (Q3 FY26) — an Indian company specialising in naval tactical trainers and submarine simulators. This gives Zen a new platform for Navy and export simulation.

**Mechanism**
- Anawave's naval simulation: Tactical Trainer and Submarine Simulators — in high demand from Indian Navy and globally.
- Synergy with ARI (previously acquired): Combined entity has Army + Air Force + Navy + submarine simulation capability.
- Export potential: Naval simulation is a highly sought-after export product (SEA, Middle East, Europe navies).

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26: "Acquired 76% stake in Anawave Systems. Set to strengthen Zen's presence in naval simulation."
- Multibagg: "Synergy with ARI expected to significantly boost combined entity's offerings in naval simulation."

**Time Horizon:** Medium term (FY27–FY29) — integration + first Anawave revenues.

---

### Driver 4: INR 6,000 Crore Revenue Target by FY28 — Long-Term Vision

**What is the driver?**
Management has maintained a cumulative revenue guidance of INR 6,000 crore by FY2028. This implies ~3x FY26 revenue in 2 years — an extremely aggressive target.

**Implied Growth Path**
- FY26 actual: INR 773 crore.
- FY27 implied: ~INR 1,500–2,000 crore.
- FY28 implied: ~INR 3,000–4,000+ crore.
- Basis: Large simulator order pipeline (INR 650 crore delayed from FY26 + new Q4 inflows of INR 431 crore) + anti-drone export ramp.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q2 FY26 call: "Company maintains its cumulative revenue guidance of INR 6,000 crore by FY2028."
- CMD Ashok Atluri: "The long-term story is intact. Companies with indigenous R&D and IP are the ones that will survive."
- Management: "Expecting stronger financial performance in FY27 due to execution of recent large orders."

**Time Horizon:** FY27–FY28 — critical execution years.

---

### Driver 5: Export Markets — EU, Middle East, Africa, Southeast Asia

**What is the driver?**
Zen's simulators and anti-drone systems are globally competitive and export-ready. Management aggressively ramping exports as domestic procurement delays create revenue volatility.

**Evidence from FY26 Official Documents**
- Q3 FY26: "Aggressively pursuing export markets in EU, Middle East, Africa, and Southeast Asia."
- Q2 FY26: 50-50 domestic/export split targeted for future revenue mix.

**Time Horizon:** Growing — first significant export revenues expected FY27.

## 5. KEY RISKS

1. **Domestic procurement dependence**: 90%+ revenue from Indian government. Procurement delays = revenue swings (as seen in FY26).
2. **FY28 INR 6,000 crore target credibility**: Requires ~8x FY26 revenue in 2 years. Extremely aggressive. Any further procurement delays → target misses badly.
3. **Q4 FY26 PAT decline**: INR 31.53 crore (-42% QoQ, -69% YoY) — sharp margin compression in Q4.
4. **Execution capacity**: Scaling from INR 773 crore to INR 6,000 crore requires massive capacity + workforce expansion.
5. **Competition from large integrators**: BEL and HAL entering simulation markets; L&T and Tata building training businesses.

**Conclusion:** Zen Technologies' FY26 is its worst financial year since listing — revenue -25%, PAT -31% — but paradoxically its best year for strategic positioning: record order book (INR 1,336 crore), largest anti-drone order (INR 245 crore), Anawave naval simulation acquisition, and Q4 FY26 order inflows of INR 431 crore alone. The INR 6,000 crore by FY28 target is aggressive but directionally supported by the deferred INR 650 crore simulator backlog + anti-drone surge + export push. FY27 is the critical test — if delayed orders convert and anti-drone exports materialise, Zen could grow 100%+ in FY27. The 48% EBITDA margin and net cash of INR 1,103 crore provide a very strong financial cushion.
