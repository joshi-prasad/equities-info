# Business Model

### a) What Does the Company Actually Do?
Shilpa Medicare is fundamentally transitioning its identity. It is shifting away from being a pure-play manufacturer of commoditized oncology Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients (APIs) and generic formulations toward becoming an integrated, differentiated healthcare platform.

The business operates across these core areas:
*   **APIs (Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients):** Developing the core chemical components for oncology and non-oncology drugs.
*   **Formulations:** Manufacturing the finished dosage forms (tablets, capsules, injectables, transdermal patches, and oral dissolving films).
*   **CDMO (Contract Development and Manufacturing Organization):** Acting as an outsourced R&D and manufacturing partner for global innovator companies, taking molecules from clinical phases through to commercial supply.
*   **Biologics & Peptides:** Developing highly complex, large-molecule biosimilars, Recombinant Human Albumin (rHA), Antibody-Drug Conjugates (ADCs), and GLP-1 peptides (Liraglutide/Semaglutide).

**Reality Check (Current vs. Narrative):**
1.  **Mature Revenue Generators:** Oncology APIs, traditional generic formulations (e.g., Pemetrexed, Bortezomib in the US; Nilotinib in Europe).
2.  **Emerging but Economically Visible:** Specialty Formulations (Nor-UDCA launch in India), CDMO services (milestone incomes from Unicycive and Orion).
3.  **Speculative Future Optionality:** Commercialization of Albumin (rHA), ADCs, and GLP-1 peptides. These are heavily promoted in the narrative but are currently in clinical or capex phases and are *not* contributing to commercial product revenues yet.

### c) Who Are the Customers?
Shilpa operates a deeply B2B model:
*   **CDMO Clients:** Innovator biotech companies (e.g., Unicycive Therapeutics for OLC, Alveolus Bio, mAbTree Biologics). These are sticky, multi-year relationships tied to clinical and commercial success.
*   **Global Licensing Partners:** Big pharma and regional specialists (e.g., Orion Corporation in Europe for Albumin, Amneal for oncology profit-sharing).
*   **Domestic Marketing Partners:** Top-10 Indian pharma companies utilized for front-end sales of first-in-class products like Nor-UDCA (Noduca™).
*   **Generic Formulators:** Buyers of Shilpa's APIs.

**Risks:** High reliance on the clinical and regulatory success of its partners. If Unicycive fails to secure FDA approval for OLC by June 2026, a major expected FY27 commercial revenue driver vanishes.

### d) How the Business Actually Works
1. **Development & Out-Licensing:** Shilpa selects complex molecules (e.g., rHA, Transdermal patches). They invest in R&D and process development.
2. **Milestone Monetization (Lumpy):** They sign deals with global partners (like Orion). Shilpa receives "licensing income" upon signing, filing, and clinical milestones.
3. **Clinical & Regulatory Validation:** The partner runs Phase 3 trials and files with regulatory agencies (e.g., EMA/USFDA).
4. **Commercial Supply (Recurring):** Once approved, Shilpa exclusively manufactures the API/Formulation, earning a supply margin and sometimes a profit-share on end sales.

*Investor Note:* Licensing income is fundamentally lumpy and unpredictable on a quarterly basis. It hit ~INR 150 cr recently, but core momentum must be judged on "ex-licensing" commercial growth.

# Value Chain

### Part 1: Linear Pharma Value Chain
Shilpa Medicare operates across an increasingly integrated, multi-layered pharmaceutical value chain:

**Raw Materials & Chemical Inputs** → **API Process Chemistry & Scale-Up** → **API Manufacturing (Captive & Merchant)** → **Finished Dosage Formulations (FDF) & Complex Delivery Systems (Transdermal/ODF)** → **Specialty CDMO / Innovator Partnerships** → **Biologics / Recombinant / Peptide Platforms** → **Regulatory Qualification & Compliance** → **Commercialization (Out-Licensing & Marketing Partnerships)** → **Post-Approval Recurring Supply**

***

### Part 2: Stage-Wise Explanation

**a) Raw Materials & Chemical Inputs**
*   **What happens:** Sourcing base chemicals, solvents, fermentation inputs, and biological substrates.
*   **Value Addition & Differentiation:** LOW. This is a commoditized stage.
*   **Economics:** Buyers are price-takers. Shilpa is actively reducing the impact of this stage on its P&L. Material costs as a percentage of sales have structurally declined from 52.9% in FY15 to 29.9% in FY26. This is hard financial evidence that the company is adding more value internally rather than just passing through raw materials.

**b) API Development & Manufacturing**
*   **What happens:** Developing process chemistry, isolating, purifying, and scaling up active pharmaceutical ingredients (Oncology, Non-Oncology, Peptides, Polymers).
*   **Value Addition & Differentiation:** MEDIUM.
*   **Economics:** Moderate margins, driven by scale and capacity utilization. Shilpa has 650 KL and 510 KL reactor capacities in Raichur.
*   **Shilpa’s Position:** This is a mature, cash-generating business. It provides steady growth but is highly competitive. Shilpa is shifting this from a merchant model (selling to others) to a captive model (feeding its own formulations) to capture higher profit pools.

**c) Formulations / Finished Dosage Forms (FDF)**
*   **What happens:** Converting APIs into patient-ready forms (tablets, injectables, transdermal patches, orally disintegrating films).
*   **Value Addition & Differentiation:** HIGH. Regulatory barriers (FDA/EMA approvals) and delivery complexity (e.g., 505(b)(2) pathways, transdermal patches) create pricing power.
*   **Economics:** High margin, high ROCE once approved. Formulators capture the lion's share of the generic profit pool.
*   **Shilpa’s Position:** This is currently driving Shilpa's earnings acceleration. The base Formulation business grew 104% QoQ and 83% YoY in 9M FY26. The launch of NorUDCA (Noduca) in India in Q3 FY26 proves they can successfully commercialize novel first-in-class products.

**d) Specialty CDMO / Innovator Partnerships**
*   **What happens:** Acting as an outsourced R&D and manufacturing partner for innovator companies (e.g., Unicycive for Oxylanthanum Carbonate).
*   **Value Addition & Differentiation:** VERY HIGH. Innovators rely on Shilpa's "clone-to-vial" or API-to-Formulation capabilities.
*   **Economics:** Extremely high switching costs. Generates lumpy "milestone" income during development, transitioning to highly predictable, long-term commercial supply margins once approved.
*   **Shilpa’s Position:** Moving from milestone/investment phase to commercial reality. CDMO generated ~INR 110 cr in API CDMO alone in FY26. However, execution risk is real: Unicycive's OLC launch was delayed to FY27 due to a third-party vendor issue, requiring Shilpa to step in to resolve CMC issues.

**e) Biologics / Recombinant / Peptide Platforms**
*   **What happens:** Developing large-molecule biosimilars, synthetic peptides (GLP-1s like Semaglutide/Liraglutide), and Recombinant Human Albumin (rHA) via fermentation.
*   **Value Addition & Differentiation:** VERY HIGH. Extreme technological and capital barriers.
*   **Economics:** Requires years of cash burn (Capex/R&D). If successful, yields massive, oligopolistic profit pools due to structural market shortages (e.g., human albumin).
*   **Shilpa’s Position:** Heavily speculative and capital-intensive. Shilpa has 200KL+ fermentation capacity. However, rHA Phase 3 trials have been repeatedly delayed (from FY26 to 1HFY27). GLP-1s are still in the DMF/validation stage. This is narrative optionality, not a current earnings driver.

**f) Regulatory Layer & Commercialization**
*   **What happens:** Securing USFDA, EU GMP, and other regulatory clearances. Partnering with larger sales forces (e.g., Orion for Europe, Amneal for US, top domestic pharma for India).
*   **Value Addition & Differentiation:** HIGH. Regulatory compliance is the ultimate gatekeeper of revenue.
*   **Shilpa’s Position:** Mixed. They successfully cleared a recent USFDA audit at Unit 2 with zero observations and received an EIR. However, they still have an unresolved Import Alert overhang on the Jadcherla formulation facility. They are executing a smart "capital light" commercial strategy by out-licensing to partners rather than building expensive front-end sales teams.

### 1. What is the NDDS Business? (The Economic Engine)
Shilpa’s NDDS segment focuses on reformulating existing, approved molecules into complex, patient-friendly delivery mechanisms. This falls heavily under the US 505(b)(2) or EU Hybrid regulatory pathways.

**The Modalities:**
*   **Transdermal Patches (TDP):** Delivering drugs through the skin (e.g., for Parkinson's or Alzheimer's).
