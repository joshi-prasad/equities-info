# Aditya Birla Capital (ABCAPITAL)

### Growth Triggers

**NBFC — Retail & MSME scaling as primary engine.** The single largest trigger in FY26 was the deliberate pivot from wholesale/corporate lending toward Retail and MSME. By Q4 FY26, the NBFC AUM reached ₹1,59,916 crore (+27% YoY), with Retail and MSME accounting for nearly **85% of incremental AUM growth** and **68% of total disbursements**. Personal and consumer loans, which had been deliberately pulled back to ~10–12% of the book in FY25, have been scaled back up to 13.4% mix, growing 60% for the full year to ₹18,738 crore disbursed. The management's proprietary B2B MSME platform, Udyog Plus, nearly doubled its AUM to ₹5,000 crore, and Baker Hughes-style deep integrations are being replicated across MSME supply chains.[^1]

**HFC — Fastest-growing housing finance franchise at scale.** Aditya Birla Housing Finance (ABHFL) posted record disbursements of ₹25,332 crore (+44% YoY) and AUM of ₹47,004 crore (+53% YoY) for FY26. An equity infusion of ₹2,750 crore from Advent International was completed in Q4 FY26, directly funding the next leg of branch expansion (100+ branches planned in FY27) and the target of ₹1 lakh crore AUM within 24–30 months.[^1]

**Life Insurance — Mix improvement and partnership expansion.** ABSLI's individual first-year premium grew 15% YoY for FY26, while the total premium rose 20% to ₹24,779 crore. The key trigger here was structural: the ULIP mix declined to 33% while traditional and protection rose to 67%, directly driving VNB margin expansion of **260 bps YoY to 20.6%**. The bancassurance channel grew 23%, supported by 12 banca tie-ups and an expanded presence in Axis Bank (from 25% to 50%+ zone coverage).[^1]

**Health Insurance — Market share acceleration.** Aditya Birla Health Insurance's gross written premium grew **39% YoY** to ₹7,292 crore, with market share in the SAHI segment rising from 12.6% to 13.7% (+110 bps). The trigger is a proprietary "Health First" model — health-engaged customers exhibit 8% lower loss ratios and 11% better persistency, creating a compounding underwriting advantage.[^1]

**AMC — AIF/PMS as new growth layer.** Mutual Fund quarterly average AUM reached ₹4.35 lakh crore (+14% YoY), but the real surprise was the 3x jump in PMS/AIF AUM from ₹11,300 crore to ₹35,700 crore in a year. The EPFO debt mandate (pending fund flows as of Q4) and the new IFSC subsidiary are structural optionalities that begin materializing in FY27.[^1]

### Revenue and Margin Evolution

Consolidated PAT (ex-one-offs) grew **30% YoY to ₹1,124 crore in Q4 FY26**; full-year consolidated PAT grew **21% YoY**. Total consolidated revenue grew 14% for FY26. NBFC PAT for the full year crossed ₹3,001 crore (+20% YoY); NBFC ROA improved **6 bps sequentially** to 2.31% in Q4. HFC PAT doubled YoY to ₹647 crore for FY26; HFC ROA reached **2.07%** in Q4 against a guidance of 2.0–2.2%.[^1]

**Margins:** NBFC NIM (including fee) was 6.08% in Q4 FY26, broadly stable. Management flagged that every 200 bps rise in unsecured mix could add 25–30 bps to NIM. Credit costs, at a record low of **1.04%** in Q4, are expected to normalize to 1.1–1.2%. Life insurance VNB margin at **20.6%** exceeded the prior guidance of 18%+; health combined ratio improved from 105% in FY25 to 103% in FY26.[^1]
