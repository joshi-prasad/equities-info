# Ajanta Pharma (AJANTPHARM)

### Growth Triggers

**US Generics — Eight new product launches driving step-change in contribution.** The US Generics business was the defining growth trigger of FY26. Full-year US sales grew **49% YoY to ₹1,557 crore**, with Q4 FY26 alone up **56% YoY to ₹505 crore**. Eight new ANDA launches over the trailing 12 months, combined with market share gains in older products and a seasonal flu product, drove the acceleration. US Generics' contribution to total revenue rose from ~19% in FY25 to **29% in FY26**.[^11][^12]

**India Branded Generics — Field force expansion and new therapies compounding.** India grew 14% for full year FY26, outpacing IPM growth by **33%** as per IQVIA MAT March 2026. The company added **~450 medical representatives** through FY26, predominantly in existing therapy areas (cardiology, ophthalmology, dermatology) to deepen coverage, and launched a new therapy (gynecology) that is gaining early traction. A GLP-1 (semaglutide) product is expected to be in the first wave of India launches (Q1 FY27) through a partnership with Biocon, leveraging the existing cardio-diabetology field force across 26 countries (23 exclusive).[^12][^11]

**Africa Branded Generics — Structural outperformance.** Africa grew 15% for FY26 (vs. initial expectations of modest single-digit growth), with Q4 FY26 up **37% YoY**. Growth was driven by 8 new product launches and improving field execution, with Africa now contributing ~16% of total revenue. Management expects Africa and Asia to be the **primary growth drivers in FY27** as US generics normalizes.[^13][^11]

**Asia — Recoverable headwind.** Asia declined 1% for full year FY26 due to logistics disruptions in the Middle East (Q4), which management attributed to geopolitical supply chain disruption rather than structural demand issues. A recovery is expected from FY27 as shipments normalize.[^11][^13]

### Revenue and Margin Evolution

| Period | Revenue | YoY | Gross Margin | EBITDA Margin | PAT Margin |
|--------|---------|-----|-------------|--------------|-----------|
| Q1 FY26 | ₹1,303 cr | +14% | 79% | 29% (ex-FX) | 20%[^14] |
| Q3 FY26 | ₹1,375 cr | +20% | 79% | 28% | 20%[^12] |
| Q4 FY26 | ₹1,422 cr | +21% | ~77% | ~27% | ~19%[^11] |
| FY26 Full Year | ₹5,453 cr | +17% | ~78% | ~27% | ~19%[^11] |

Gross margins have benefited from better product mix (US generics at higher margin, favorable API prices) and are expected to settle at **~77% (±1%)** in FY27 due to rising freight and raw material costs. EBITDA margins track to guidance of **27% (±1%)**. The personal cost line is under pressure (+17–25% YoY) from the MR additions and a ₹7 crore new labor code provision.[^12][^11]

**Capital allocation:** FY26 capex of ₹300 crore (in line with guidance); management is evaluating acquisitions with up to ₹1,000 crore+ firepower, signaling possible inorganic expansion in FY27.[^12]
