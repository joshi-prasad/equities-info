## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: 5 GW+ Capacity Addition — Highest Globally (Ex-China)

**FY26 capacity additions:**
| Segment | FY26 Addition (MW) | Cumulative FY26 (MW) |
|---------|--------------------|----------------------|
| Solar | 3,412 (incl. 2,974 MW Khavda) | 13,515 |
| Wind | 683 (Khavda) | 2,563 |
| Hybrid | 956 (Khavda) | 3,216 |
| **Total** | **5,051** | **19,294** |

9M FY26 greenfield addition: 3 GW vs only 0.4 GW in 9M FY25 — **7.5x acceleration**.  
Each GW commissioned adds approximately ₹500–700 Cr of annualised PPA revenue.

### Driver 2: Khavda Mega-Site — 9.4 GW Operational, 30 GW Target by 2029

- Khavda is the **world's largest single-location renewable energy plant** at 9.4 GW.
- Grid connectivity via 765 kV transmission + green corridor.
- Target: **30 GW at Khavda by 2029** (3.2x scale-up from current).
- Scale creates cost advantages: shared O&M, shared transmission, negotiating leverage.

### Driver 3: BESS — 1,376 MWh Commissioned, 10 GWh Target FY27

- 1,376 MWh commissioned at Khavda in 8 months from groundbreaking — exceptional speed.
- Contract mix: **75% long-term PPAs + 25% merchant/C&I**.
- Target: **>10 GWh BESS by FY27** — 7x current base.
- Enables firm power supply, capturing higher tariffs and reducing curtailment risk.

### Driver 4: 91–94% EBITDA Margin — Operating Leverage at Scale

- FY25: 91.7% | 9M FY26: 91.5% | Q3 FY26: 90.6% | FY26 full year: ~93.6%
- Driven by ENOC proprietary O&M, fixed-price 25-year PPAs, and fixed-cost leverage.
- Cash profit ₹4,893 Cr (+21%) funds further capacity from internal accruals.

### Driver 5: 50 GW by 2030 — Structural Revenue Visibility

- From 19.3 GW today → 50 GW by 2030 = ~8 GW additions/year required.
- FY26 proved AGEL can deliver 5 GW in a single year; target requires acceleration.
- **Revenue potential at 50 GW:** ₹25,000–30,000 Cr (vs ₹11,602 Cr in FY26).
- AGEL ranked **#1 Green Utility Worldwide** by Energy Intelligence (2025).

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. **Grid curtailment:** Khavda-scale additions outpacing grid absorption; curtailments cut actual generation.
2. **Silver price:** Rising silver cost impacts PV capex for new projects.
3. **Merchant price volatility:** ~20% merchant revenue exposed to soft power prices.
4. **Interest burden:** Large debt load; partly hedged on forex.
5. **Execution at scale:** 5 GW/year requires flawless multi-state coordination.
6. **Adani Group overhang:** Financing cost and sentiment risk.
