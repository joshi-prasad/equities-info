## 1. COMPANY PROFILE

Waaree Energies Limited is India's largest solar module manufacturer and the world's largest non-Chinese solar module manufacturer (26 GW capacity). FY26: Revenue INR 26,537 Cr (+84% YoY), Operating EBITDA INR 5,909 Cr (+117% YoY, 22.27% margin), PAT INR 3,884 Cr (+101% YoY). Total EBITDA (reported): INR 6,617 Cr — surpassed FY26 guidance of INR 5,500–6,000 Cr. ROCE: 32.4%. ROE: 29%. FY27 EBITDA guided: INR 7,000–7,700 Cr.

---

## 2. CORE GROWTH DRIVERS

### Driver 1: Module Capacity Scale + G12R/TOPCon Technology Upgrade

**What is the driver?**
FY26 module production: 12.6 GW (+77% YoY). G12R transition: 580W → 615W modules = 10–12% higher wattage and realisation at same cell cost. 3 of 11 lines converted in Q4 FY26; 5 remaining converting Q1 FY27. Additional 3 GW capacity commissioned at Samakhiali, Gujarat in Q4 FY26.

**Evidence (Official FY26 Call, April 30, 2026)**
- CEO Jignesh Rathod: "Total module capacity: ~26 GW — largest non-Chinese globally."
- CFO Abhishek Pareek: "Module production Q4 FY26: 4.2 GW (+104% YoY). FY26 total: 12.6 GW (+77% YoY)."
- CFO: "G12R — 3 lines converted. 10–12% higher efficiency and realization from G12R."

---

### Driver 2: Cell Capacity Ramp — 5.4 GW In-House, H2 FY27 Margin Expansion

FY26 cell production: 2.3 GW (vs 0.1 GW FY25) — still ramping. DCR modules at INR 21–22/Wp vs non-DCR INR 15–16/Wp. H2 FY27: In-house cells cover majority of module requirements → gross margin expansion.

**Evidence**
- CEO: "Cell manufacturing capacity: 5.4 GW — largest in India."
- CFO: "Q4 margin pressure partly from lower DCR mix — cells lagged modules. H2 FY27 cell ramp drives margin improvement."

---

### Driver 3: US Manufacturing — 1.6 GW Operational, Expanding to 4.2 GW (Q2 FY27)

1.6 GW US facility built in 12 months — one of fastest greenfield solar builds globally. India cells face 123% ADD+CVD in US; US manufacturing = zero tariff exposure. Ethiopian cells used for US-bound modules = 10% tariff only. FEOC-compliant → IRA tax credits eligible.

**Evidence**
- CEO: "Built 1.6 GW in the US in just 12 months. On track to expand to 4.2 GW over next six months."
- CFO: "4.2 GW US local capacity insulates us from import duties."

## 4. KEY RISKS

1. Q4 FY26 margin dip: ~18.6% vs 25.49% Q3. Causes: silver/copper costs; logistics; lower overseas mix; G12R transitions.
2. Capex execution: INR 30,000 Cr committed across multiple verticals simultaneously.
3. US tariff escalation risk.
4. INR 10,000 Cr QIP dilution.
5. Cell ramp timeline: H2 FY27 ramp subject to equipment commissioning.
